Never used AI? You'll be a pro in 5 minutes.

No jargon. Think of AI as a brilliant, tireless teaching assistant you talk to like a text message. Let's do your very first one together.

What is this, really?

AI tools like ChatGPT are websites where you type a request (or attach a photo) and get a written answer back — like chatting with a super-fast assistant. You do not install anything or write any code.

📱

What do I need?

Just a phone or computer with internet, and a free account on one AI tool (we'll open it together below). That's it. No payment needed to start.

✅

Will it actually work?

Text tasks (papers, worksheets, solutions, notes, books) work on any free AI. Picture tasks (the handwritten art pages) need an image-making AI and free limits apply — we tell you on every prompt.

How to attach a photo of a question

This is the one step everyone asks about. It is genuinely easy — here is exactly where to tap. Choose your device:

  1. Open the AI app or website and start a new chat.
  2. In the typing bar at the bottom, tap the + button (in some apps it's a paperclip 📎 or camera icon) on the left.
  3. A small menu appears. Choose "Take Photo" to click the question now, or "Photo Library / Gallery" to pick a photo you already took.
  4. Select the photo. You'll see a small picture appear above the typing bar — that means it's attached. ✓
  5. Now paste the prompt (next step), then tap the send arrow.
Tip: tap the AI's name (ChatGPT / Gemini) at the top to make sure you're in a chat, not on a settings screen. The + / paperclip always sits next to where you type.
  1. Open the AI website in your browser and start a new chat.
  2. Click the paperclip 📎 (or +) icon next to the message box, then choose "Upload from computer" and pick your photo file.
  3. Even easier: just drag the image file from your folder straight onto the message box, or copy an image and press Ctrl + V (Windows) / Cmd + V (Mac).
  4. A small thumbnail appears in the box — it's attached. ✓
  5. Paste the prompt, then press Enter (or click the send arrow).
No photo file yet? Take a picture on your phone, then send it to your computer via WhatsApp Web / email, save it, and drag it in. Or just do the whole thing on your phone — it works the same.

How to copy a prompt from here and paste it

  1. In the Prompt Library below, find a prompt and tap "Copy prompt". It says "Copied!" — the whole instruction is now copied.
  2. Go back to your AI chat. Tap-and-hold the typing bar, then choose Paste (phone), or press Ctrl + V / Cmd + V (computer).
  3. If the prompt needed a photo, make sure you attached it first (Step 2), then paste.
  4. Replace any [SQUARE BRACKETS] with your details (your class, board, topic).
  5. Press Send / Enter. Done! Read it, then download or screenshot the result.

5 habits that make the AI work brilliantly

  • One question per photo. Crop or cover the rest so the AI focuses on what you want.
  • Good light, flat page, straight angle. A clear photo = correct maths.
  • Always fill the [BRACKETS] with your class, board and topic before sending.
  • Just talk back to it. "Make it simpler", "add one more method", "write bigger", "do it in Hindi" — it understands plain requests.
  • Glance at the answer before class. Every prompt asks the AI to double-check, but your expert eye is the final word.

Help & troubleshooting

If something doesn't work, read this

I can't find the + or paperclip button to attach a photo.

It always sits right next to where you type, at the bottom. On a phone it may be a small +; tap it and choose "Photos" or "Camera". If you truly don't see it, you might be on a settings page — tap "New chat" first. On some free plans, photo upload appears only after you've signed in. Gemini and Claude show it for free users; on ChatGPT, sign in and look for the + on the left of the message bar.

It gave me text, but I wanted the handwritten image pages.

Making images is different from writing text. Use a prompt marked "Makes images" (amber dot) and an image-capable AI (ChatGPT or Gemini). If it still replies with text, say: "Please generate this as an actual image, not text." On free plans there's a daily image limit — if it stops, try again tomorrow or use Gemini.

It only made one page, not five.

That's normal — many tools make one image per turn. Just reply "now generate Page 2", then "Page 3", and so on. The prompt already tells the AI to keep the same style across all pages.

The AI misread my question or made a mistake.

Two fixes: (1) send a clearer, brighter photo of just that one question; (2) reply with the "If it's wrong, say this" line printed on each prompt card, e.g. "Re-check step 3 and verify the final answer a second way." Your expert eye is the final check.

Can I use this for my coaching / school?

Yes, absolutely. Make solutions, papers, DPPs, worksheets and books for your students freely. Please keep the signature line when you share outputs.

Can it work in Hindi or another language?

Yes. Add "Write everything in Hindi" (or any language) to the end of any prompt and the AI will follow.

Which prompts should a complete beginner start with?

Start with the green-dot "Text only" prompts — Single Perfect Solution, Question Papers, Worksheets, Formula Sheets. They work on every free AI, no image-making needed. Once comfortable, try the amber-dot handwritten art styles.
