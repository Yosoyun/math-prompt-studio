Complete beginner's guide

Never used AI? You'll be a pro in 5 minutes.

No jargon. Think of AI as a brilliant, tireless teaching assistant you talk to like a text message. Let's do your very first one together.

🧠

What is this, really?

AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude are websites where you type a request (or attach a photo) and get a written answer back — like chatting with a super-fast assistant. You do not install anything or write any code.

📱

What do I need?

Just a phone or computer with internet, and a free account on one AI tool (we'll open it together below). No payment needed to start.

✅

Will it actually work?

Text tasks (papers, worksheets, solutions, notes) work on any free AI. Picture tasks (the handwritten art pages) need an image-making AI and free limits apply — we tell you on every prompt.

How to attach a photo of a question

This is the one step everyone asks about. It is genuinely easy — here is exactly where to tap. Choose your device:

  1. Open the AI app or website and start a new chat.
  2. In the typing bar at the bottom, tap the + button (sometimes a paperclip 📎 or camera) on the left.
  3. Choose "Take Photo" to click the question now, or "Photo Library / Gallery" for one you already took.
  4. Select the photo — a small picture appears above the bar = attached. ✓
  5. Now paste the prompt (next step), then tap the send arrow.
Tip: if you don't see the +, tap "New chat" first. In ChatGPT and Claude the + / paperclip sits right next to where you type.
  1. Open the AI website and start a new chat.
  2. Click the paperclip 📎 (or +) next to the message box, then "Upload from computer" and pick your photo.
  3. Even easier: drag the image onto the message box, or copy it and press Ctrl + V / Cmd + V.
  4. A small thumbnail appears = attached. ✓
  5. Paste the prompt, then press Enter.
No photo file yet? Take it on your phone, send it to yourself on WhatsApp / email, save it, and drag it in — or just do everything on the phone.
3

How to copy a prompt and paste it

  1. In the Prompt Library, tap "Copy prompt". It says "Copied!" — the whole instruction is copied.
  2. Go to your AI chat. Tap-and-hold the bar and choose Paste (phone), or Ctrl + V / Cmd + V (computer).
  3. If it needs a photo, attach it first (Step 2), then paste.
  4. Replace any [SQUARE BRACKETS] with your details (class, board, topic).
  5. Press Send / Enter. Read it, then use it in class.

Help & troubleshooting

If something doesn't work, read this

I can't find the + or paperclip button to attach a photo.

It always sits right next to where you type, at the bottom. Tap it and choose "Photos" or "Camera". If you don't see it, tap "New chat" first and make sure you're signed in. In ChatGPT and Claude the + / paperclip is on the left of the message bar.

It gave me text, but I wanted the handwritten image pages.

Making images is different from writing text. Use a prompt marked "Makes images" (amber dot) and an image-capable AI (ChatGPT or Gemini / AI Studio). If it still replies with text, say: "Please generate this as an actual image, not text." Free plans limit images per day.

It only made one page, not five.

Normal — many tools make one image per turn. Reply "now generate Page 2", then "Page 3", and so on. The prompt keeps the same style across pages.

The AI misread my question or made a mistake.

Two fixes: send a clearer, brighter photo of just that one question; or reply with the "If it's wrong, say this" line on each prompt card, e.g. "Re-check step 3 and verify the answer a second way." Your expert eye is the final check.

Is there a fee? How can I support this?

It's completely free — no fee, nothing to buy, nothing to download. The best way to support it is to share it with one more teacher or student, and to send feedback so it keeps improving.

Why does "Indrajeet Yadav" appear on the results?

Each prompt asks the AI to sign the work — a handwritten signature on image pages, or a "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav" line on papers. It's how this is shared freely while keeping the author's name with his work. Use everything in your classes happily.

Can students use this too?

Yes — especially the Learn 10× section. Any student who wants to study maths smarter can use these techniques with ChatGPT or Claude for free.

Can it work in Hindi or another language?

Yes. Add "Write everything in Hindi" (or any language) to the end of any prompt.
