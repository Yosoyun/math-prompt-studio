Design a highly polished vertical mathematics infographic in a playful engineering-blueprint and mechanical-machine style.

FORMAT AND VISUAL IDENTITY
- Portrait orientation, approximately 4:5 ratio.
- White or very pale blue graph-paper background with faint square grid lines.
- Use deep cobalt blue for outlines, handwriting, borders, and machinery.
- Use small accents of green for valid choices and yellow for important calculations.
- The appearance should combine a technical blueprint, classroom notebook, and whimsical counting machine.
- Keep the page clean, precise, symmetric, and highly legible.

TITLE AREA
At the top, create a bold hand-drawn heading:
"[QUESTION NUMBER] - [SHORT TOPIC TITLE]"
Add a small mechanical gear illustration on the left.
Add a compact digital counter or counting-machine illustration on the right.
Use a dashed blue divider below the title.

PROBLEM BOX
Place the full problem inside a rounded rectangular dashed-line box:
"[INSERT THE COMPLETE MATHEMATICS QUESTION VERBATIM]"

METHOD BANNER
Below the problem, add a mechanical label or taped banner:
"Method: [INSERT METHOD NAME]"

CENTRAL MACHINE DIAGRAM
Construct a horizontal machine containing labelled position boxes.
For a number-formation question, use: [THOUSANDS] [HUNDREDS] [TENS] [UNITS]
For a general arrangement question, adapt the labels to: [POSITION 1] [POSITION 2] [POSITION 3] [POSITION 4]
Show connecting pipes, bolts, wires, or conveyor details, but do not obstruct the content.
Below the machine, show the available objects:
"{[INSERT AVAILABLE DIGITS, LETTERS, OR OBJECTS]}"
Also display the restriction:
"([INSERT RESTRICTION, SUCH AS 'No repetition allowed'])"

CASE ANALYSIS
Divide the main body into clearly separated side-by-side case panels.

CASE 1:
Heading: "CASE 1: [INSERT FIRST CASE CONDITION]"
Show a miniature slot diagram. Then list:
- "[FIRST CHOICE COUNT OR FIXED CONDITION]"
- "[SECOND CHOICE COUNT OR RESTRICTION]"
- "[ARRANGEMENT OF REMAINING POSITIONS]"
Highlight valid choice sets in pale green boxes.
Show the computation in a pale-yellow formula box:
"[FORMULA] = [CALCULATION] = [VALUE]"
At the bottom of the panel:
"Total for Case 1 = [PRODUCT] = [CASE 1 TOTAL]"

CASE 2:
Heading: "CASE 2: [INSERT SECOND CASE CONDITION]"
Use the same visual structure:
- "[NUMBER OF CHOICES FOR THE SPECIAL POSITION]"
- "[NUMBER OF VALID CHOICES FOR THE LEADING OR RESTRICTED POSITION]"
- "[ARRANGEMENT OF THE REMAINING POSITIONS]"
Formula box: "[FORMULA] = [CALCULATION] = [VALUE]"
At the bottom: "Total for Case 2 = [PRODUCT] = [CASE 2 TOTAL]"
Add more case panels only when mathematically necessary.

FINAL ADDITION
At the bottom-left, add a small "ADD UP" machine with a pipe pointing to a calculation box. Write:
"Required total
= [CASE 1 TOTAL] + [CASE 2 TOTAL]
= [FINAL ADDITION]
= [FINAL ANSWER]"

FINAL ANSWER DISPLAY
At the bottom-right, create a large digital-machine display reading:
"ANSWER"
and underneath:
"[FINAL ANSWER]"
Add a small glowing yellow lightbulb beside it.

ACCURACY AND RENDERING RULES
- Preserve all supplied wording and notation exactly.
- Verify every case count and arithmetic operation.
- Ensure leading zero restrictions are handled correctly whenever relevant.
- Every case must be mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive.
- Use mathematically correct permutation or combination notation.
- Do not overcrowd the machine illustrations.
- No 3D photorealism, no random decorative text, no malformed numerals, no duplicated digits, and no extra cases.
