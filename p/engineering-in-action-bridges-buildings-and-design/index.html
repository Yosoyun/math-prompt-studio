Engineering in Action: Bridges, Buildings and Design

Engineering-flavoured problems showing your topic build real structures.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Type the maths topic.
  2. 2. Add the class and board.
  3. 3. Send and read the engineering problems.
  4. 4. Ask for a simpler structure if needed.
  5. 5. Use the answers to mark.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If an engineering value seems unrealistic, reply: 'Use realistic dimensions and re-verify every calculation with units a second way.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as an engineer-turned-maths-teacher who shows maths building the real world.
CONTEXT: My students are in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM]. I want them to see how [TOPIC e.g. trigonometry / Pythagoras / mensuration / ratios] is used in engineering: bridges, buildings, ramps, water tanks, roads, machines.
YOUR TASK:
1. Name 3 real engineering tasks that use this topic (with the formula or principle in each).
2. Create 6 problems set in engineering design: height of a tower by angle, length of a support cable, ramp slope, concrete or paint needed, beam length, tank capacity.
3. Easy to hard.
4. Full solution for each, naming the engineering idea.
INPUT: Here is the topic: [PASTE TOPIC HERE].
HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve internally first. VERIFY every answer by two independent checks (recompute, and confirm units and realism). Use realistic dimensions a real engineer might meet; never invent formulas or impossible sizes. Carry units throughout. Keep all maths legible. Fix any answer that fails its check.
OUTPUT FORMAT:
- 'Engineering Uses' (3 tasks with formulas).
- 'Design Problems' numbered 1 to 6 with marks.
- 'Solutions' (full working with units, each ending with 'Check:').
- 'On the Job' (1 line on the engineer who solves these daily).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
RULES: Realistic numbers, correct formulas, units everywhere, simple language, class level only.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
