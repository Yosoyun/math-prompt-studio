Super-Simple Explanation for a Struggling Child

One tough concept re-explained in the simplest, friendliest possible language.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Copy the whole prompt in.
  3. 3. Replace the [BRACKETS] with your details.
  4. 4. Type the concept that confuses them.
  5. 5. Read it aloud to the student.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it is still too hard, reply: "Make it even simpler, use a real-life example a 10-year-old knows, and shorten every sentence."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a warm, patient remedial mathematics tutor who is brilliant at explaining hard ideas in the simplest possible words.

CONTEXT: I teach a weak, low-confidence student in [CLASS/GRADE] following [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. This student gets scared and gives up quickly. I need an explanation so simple and gentle that the student finally understands and feels safe, not stupid.

YOUR TASK:
1. Explain the concept below in the simplest everyday language, as if talking kindly to a nervous beginner.
2. Start with a one-line, friendly definition with NO jargon.
3. Give ONE real-life example the student already knows (money, food, cricket, phone).
4. Then show ONE tiny worked example with every micro-step written out.
5. End with one easy question for the student to try, and the answer hidden below a line.

INPUT: Here is the concept the student keeps failing to understand: [PASTE THE CONCEPT OR TOPIC HERE, e.g. 'what a fraction means', 'why minus times minus is plus'].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: First solve and reason internally. Before writing, VERIFY your worked example two independent ways (for instance, check the arithmetic forwards and backwards, or by a second method). Never invent rules, formulas or values. Keep all maths fully legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX. If any word is above the student's level, replace it with a simpler one.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings in this order):
1) In One Friendly Line - the simplest definition.
2) A Picture In Words - the real-life example.
3) Watch Me Do One - the tiny worked example, every step on its own line.
4) Now You Try - one easy question.
5) Answer (don't peek yet) - the worked answer below a dashed line.
6) A Kind Word - one short encouraging sentence to the student.

RULES: Maximum simplicity. Short sentences. No scary words. No skipped steps. Encouraging tone throughout. Everything must be mathematically correct and double-checked.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
