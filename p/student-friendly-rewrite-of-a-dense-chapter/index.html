Student-Friendly Rewrite of a Dense Chapter

A heavy chapter rewritten in simple, encouraging, easy-to-read language.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Paste the difficult chapter text.
  2. 2. State the class and board.
  3. 3. Send the prompt.
  4. 4. Check the maths is unchanged.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it got too simple and lost rigour, reply: "Keep it friendly but restore the full method in Section 2; do not drop any required step."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics author who rewrites dense material in a friendly voice.

CONTEXT: I have a chapter that is correct but too dense and dry for my [CLASS/GRADE] students of [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. The topic is [TOPIC]. I want a student-friendly rewrite that keeps all the mathematics but is easier and more encouraging to read.

YOUR TASK:
1. Rewrite the chapter in simple, warm, encouraging language.
2. Break long passages into short paragraphs and clear sub-headings.
3. Add brief signposts like "Why this matters" and "In simple words".
4. Keep every formula, method, and example mathematically identical.
5. Where a step is hard, add one plain-language explanation beside it.
6. Do not remove any required step or simplify away the rigour.

INPUT: Here is the chapter to rewrite: [PASTE THE CHAPTER TEXT HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Preserve the exact mathematics. VERIFY two ways before finishing: (a) confirm every formula, value, and result matches the original, and (b) re-read to confirm no required step was dropped in the name of simplicity. If the original has an error, flag it under "Points To Confirm" rather than silently changing it. Keep all mathematics in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1. The Rewritten Chapter (friendly tone, clear headings, same maths)
2. "Points To Confirm" (any suspected error in the original, listed separately)

RULES: Friendlier wording only; mathematics unchanged. No dropped steps. Short paragraphs and signposts. Flag, do not fix, suspected errors.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
