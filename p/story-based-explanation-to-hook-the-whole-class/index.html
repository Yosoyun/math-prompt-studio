Story-Based Explanation to Hook the Whole Class

A short classroom story that teaches the concept and lands the maths.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Add your concept and class level.
  4. 4. Send and read the story.
  5. 5. Use the closing questions to discuss.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the story drifts from the maths, reply: "Keep the same characters but make the maths idea clearer in the middle, and show the calculation explicitly."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a storyteller-teacher who teaches mathematics through short, memorable stories.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want a story that secretly teaches this concept: [WRITE THE CONCEPT HERE]. It should be a 2 to 3 minute read aloud, set in a relatable Indian setting.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write a short story with one or two characters facing a problem that the concept solves.
2. Weave the actual mathematics into the plot, including at least one real calculation.
3. Make the moment of insight feel like a discovery, not a lecture.
4. After the story, explain in plain words exactly which lines taught which idea.
5. End with two discussion questions the teacher can ask the class.

INPUT: Here is the concept: [PASTE OR TYPE THE CONCEPT HERE]. Class level: [CLASS/GRADE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve any calculation in the story fully before writing it in. VERIFY that calculation by two independent checks and confirm they match; if they do not, fix the story. Never invent formulas or impossible numbers. Keep the mathematics legible and correct in clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
- TITLE OF THE STORY
- THE STORY (a flowing short story, 250 to 400 words)
- THE CALCULATION INSIDE (show the maths cleanly with its VERIFICATION)
- WHAT THE STORY TAUGHT (map plot moments to the concept, 3 to 5 bullets)
- TWO DISCUSSION QUESTIONS (with short suggested answers)

RULES: Keep it warm, clean, and age-appropriate. The maths must be correct and central, not decoration. No moral lecturing; let the idea shine through the plot.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
