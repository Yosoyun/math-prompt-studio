Mixed-Bag Revision Sheet (Whole-Term Recap)

A varied revision sheet mixing formats and chapters for a full-term recap, with answer key.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. List the whole term's chapters.
  2. 2. Add class and board.
  3. 3. Send the prompt.
  4. 4. Check the format variety.
  5. 5. Print the revision sheet.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it feels repetitive, reply: "Add more variety: include at least 2 MCQs, 2 fill-in-blanks, and 2 short word problems, and re-verify all answers."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who builds varied mixed-format revision sheets before tests.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board]. Before the term test I want ONE revision sheet that mixes question formats and covers all chapters of the term, so students get an all-round recap.

YOUR TASK:
1. Cover these chapters: [LIST TERM CHAPTERS HERE].
2. Create 18 questions using a deliberate MIX of formats: some MCQs (4 options each), some fill-in-the-blanks, some one-line short answers, some 2-3 step problems, and 2 short word problems.
3. Spread the chapters fairly and label each question with its chapter, e.g. [Ch: Algebra].
4. Order roughly easy to hard. Show marks per question and the total.

INPUT: Here are the term chapters and any to emphasise: [PASTE CHAPTERS AND NOTES HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve every question internally first; for MCQs confirm exactly one option is correct and the distractors are wrong. VERIFY each final answer two independent ways. Never invent formulas or values. Keep all maths fully legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):
- TITLE BLOCK: 'Term Revision - Mixed Bag', Class, Chapters covered, Total Marks, and 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
- QUESTIONS: Q1-Q18, each tagged with chapter, format, and marks.
- ANSWER KEY: final answers Q1-Q18 (for MCQs give the correct option letter) clearly separated at the end.

RULES: Genuine variety of formats. Fair chapter spread. MCQs have exactly one correct option. Answer key must match. Simple, clear wording.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
