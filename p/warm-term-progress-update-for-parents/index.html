Warm Term Progress Update for Parents

A balanced, kind progress message ready to send to one parent.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the whole prompt below.
  3. 3. Fill the [BRACKETS] with real details.
  4. 4. Read it once before you send.
  5. 5. Ask for a shorter version if needed.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it sounds too generic, reply: "Add the 2 specific things I mentioned and remove vague praise."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a warm, experienced school maths teacher writing to a parent.

CONTEXT: I teach [SUBJECT e.g. Mathematics] to my student [STUDENT NAME], who is in [CLASS/GRADE] following [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want to send a short, honest, encouraging progress update to the parent for this [TERM/MONTH]. The parent is busy and may not be a maths person, so keep it simple and human.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write one progress-update message a parent will actually enjoy reading.
2. Mention 2 genuine strengths and 1 area to improve, based only on the facts I give.
3. Suggest 1 small, doable step the parent can support at home.
4. End on an encouraging, partnership tone.

INPUT - here are the real details (do not invent any others):
- Strengths I noticed: [STRENGTH 1], [STRENGTH 2]
- One thing to improve: [WEAK AREA e.g. careless sign errors / skipping steps]
- A recent example or score: [TEST/HOMEWORK + RESULT, optional]
- Tone I want: [warm / formal / very friendly]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Use ONLY the facts above; never invent grades, marks, or incidents. If a detail is missing, write a neutral placeholder in [brackets] for me to fill, rather than guessing. Keep every sentence clear and free of jargon. Re-read your draft once and remove anything that could sound harsh or accusatory.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
SUBJECT LINE: one friendly line.
MESSAGE: 120 to 160 words, in short paragraphs, suitable to copy into email or WhatsApp.
ONE-LINE SUMMARY (for me): a single sentence I can note in my records.

RULES:
- Honest but kind; no exaggeration, no false praise.
- No technical maths jargon a parent would not understand.
- Indian-school-appropriate, respectful tone; address the parent politely.
- Plain, clean text only.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
