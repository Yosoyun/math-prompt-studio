Doubt-Clearing Reel From a Student Question Photo

A short reel script that answers one real student doubt clearly and quickly.

Attach a photo of the question first  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat that reads images (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open an AI chat that accepts photos.
  2. 2. Tap the attach icon and add the photo.
  3. 3. Paste the prompt below.
  4. 4. Record the spoken answer.
  5. 5. Ask it to re-read the photo if misread.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it misreads the question, reply: "You misread the problem; it actually says [correct text]. Redo the solution and verify again."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a patient maths teacher who turns one student doubt into a crisp doubt-clearing reel.

CONTEXT: A student asked a doubt and I have attached a photo of it. The reel is for [CLASS/GRADE] students of [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. It should be 40 to 60 seconds, spoken to camera, and resolve exactly the confusion shown.

YOUR TASK:
1. Read the attached photo carefully and restate the exact question in words.
2. Name the likely point of confusion in one line.
3. Give the clear, correct resolution with one worked step-by-step solution.
4. End with a one-line tip so the student never makes this mistake again.

INPUT: I have attached a photo of the student's doubt/question. If anything in the photo is unclear, tell me what you cannot read before solving.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: First transcribe the question from the photo and show me your reading. Solve it fully, then VERIFY the final answer by two independent methods. Never guess unreadable values; ask me instead. Keep all maths legible in plain text or LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (in this order):
WHAT I READ: your transcription of the question.
HOOK (0-5s): one spoken line naming the doubt.
THE CONFUSION (5-15s): the exact misunderstanding in plain words.
THE FIX (15-50s): step-by-step spoken solution; on-screen maths in [BRACKETS].
FINAL ANSWER: clearly boxed.
TIP (50-60s): one-line prevention tip.
VERIFICATION NOTE: how the answer was checked two ways.

RULES: Resolve only the doubt shown, do not wander. If the photo is unclear, stop and ask. Maths must be flawless and legible. Keep spoken words under 130.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Footer: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.
