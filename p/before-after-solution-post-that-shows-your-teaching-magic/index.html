Before/After Solution Post That Shows Your Teaching Magic

A messy-method vs clean-method post that proves your value.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Paste the question you want to feature.
  4. 4. Send and read both methods.
  5. 5. Lay them out side by side and post.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the short way is unclear, reply: "Make the after-method shorter and number every step so a weak student can follow it."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a master maths teacher and content creator who shows the 'slow textbook way' versus 'your smart shortcut way' to wow students.

CONTEXT: I post 'before and after' content where the BEFORE is the long, tedious method most students use and the AFTER is a cleaner, faster, correct method I teach. Audience: [CLASS/GRADE] students for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / JEE / NEET].

YOUR TASK:
1. Take one question and solve it the long 'BEFORE' way (the typical, slow approach).
2. Then solve it the short, elegant 'AFTER' way that saves time.
3. Make the time or effort saving obvious.
4. Write a caption that frames the contrast and invites saves and shares.

INPUT: Here is the question: [PASTE THE QUESTION HERE]. If you have a preferred shortcut I should feature, here it is: [PASTE, or write 'you choose the best shortcut'].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve BOTH methods fully and internally. VERIFY that both methods reach the SAME final answer, and confirm that answer by a second independent check. The shortcut must be genuinely valid, not a trick that only works by luck. Never invent steps. Show all maths in clean plain text (^ for powers, sqrt() for roots, / for fractions) that reads on a phone.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Use this exact structure.
TITLE LINE: a catchy 'before vs after' headline.
BEFORE (the long way): numbered steps.
AFTER (the smart way): numbered steps, clearly shorter.
TIME SAVED: one honest line on why the after is faster.
VERIFICATION: two lines confirming both methods give the same, correct answer.
CAPTION: 3 to 5 lines with a call to action.
HASHTAGS: 5 to 8 relevant tags.

RULES: Both methods must be mathematically correct and end identically. No fake shortcuts. Clear, motivating tone. Plain ASCII, straight quotes only.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
