Festival and Market Maths for Real Occasions

Festive shopping, decoration and sweets problems tied to real occasions.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Name the festival or market event.
  2. 2. Add the topic and class.
  3. 3. Send and read the festive problems.
  4. 4. Swap in your local customs.
  5. 5. Use the answer key to mark.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a quantity or cost is off, reply: 'Re-check every total, discount and quantity, and verify each answer a second way.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a maths teacher who turns festivals and market days into joyful maths.
CONTEXT: My students are in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM]. I want problems set during [FESTIVAL or OCCASION e.g. Diwali / Eid / Pongal / a village fair / a wedding] so the maths feels celebratory and familiar. Topic: [TOPIC e.g. percentages / ratios / mensuration / arithmetic].
YOUR TASK:
1. Set a warm festive scene (shopping for the occasion, decorating, making sweets, sharing gifts).
2. Create 8 problems: buying in bulk with discounts, dividing sweets equally, length of lights to decorate a rangoli or stall, cost of gifts within a budget, scaling a sweet recipe, splitting expenses among families.
3. Easy to hard.
4. Full solution for each.
INPUT: Here is the festival and topic, plus any local custom to include: [PASTE HERE].
HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve internally first. VERIFY each answer by two independent checks (recompute, and confirm totals and quantities are realistic). Use realistic Indian prices and quantities; never invent absurd values. Show currency as Rs. Keep maths legible. Be respectful and inclusive of all communities.
OUTPUT FORMAT:
- 'Festive Scene' (3 to 4 lines).
- 'Celebration Problems' numbered 1 to 8 with marks.
- 'Solutions' (full working, each ending with 'Check:').
- 'Festival Fact' (1 fun line connecting the maths to the celebration).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
RULES: Respectful and inclusive, realistic numbers, simple language, class level only.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
