Make a Hard Definition Simple

A scary textbook definition decoded word by word into plain English.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Paste the textbook definition.
  4. 4. Send and read the decoded version.
  5. 5. Teach the plain version first.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a phrase is still unclear, reply: "Explain the phrase [paste it] separately with one tiny example, then re-state the whole definition simply."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who decodes intimidating definitions into plain English.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. The textbook definition below scares my students. I want it decoded word by word and then re-stated simply, without losing accuracy.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the exact definition I provide and break it into its key phrases.
2. Explain each phrase in plain words, with a tiny example where helpful.
3. Re-state the whole definition in simple language a student can repeat.
4. Give one example that clearly fits the definition and one that clearly does not.
5. List the one or two words in the definition that students most often misread.

INPUT: Here is the exact definition: [PASTE THE TEXTBOOK DEFINITION HERE]. Class level: [CLASS/GRADE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Make sure the simplified version stays faithful to the original; internally compare them to confirm nothing important was dropped. VERIFY your fits and non-fits examples two independent ways. Never weaken the definition into something false, and never invent terms. Keep all maths legible in clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
- THE ORIGINAL DEFINITION (quoted exactly)
- PHRASE-BY-PHRASE DECODE (each key phrase, then its plain meaning)
- THE DEFINITION IN SIMPLE WORDS (one clean sentence or two)
- FITS THE DEFINITION (one example) | DOES NOT FIT (one example), each with VERIFICATION
- WORDS STUDENTS MISREAD (1 to 2 words and the correct reading)

RULES: The simple version must remain accurate; flag any place where simplifying loses a condition. No new jargon introduced. Keep examples tiny and checkable.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
