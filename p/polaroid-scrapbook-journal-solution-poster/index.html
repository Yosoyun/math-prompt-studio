Polaroid Scrapbook Journal Solution Poster

A warm handmade scrapbook page with taped Polaroids, washi tape, and journal handwriting holding the solution.

Makes images - needs an image AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT Plus (image) or Gemini

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Paste the problem verbatim or attach a clear photo
  2. Ask it to solve and double-check before rendering
  3. Request maximum detail and legible handwriting
  4. Verify the digits and that no condition was dropped
  5. If it looks messy or wrong, reply with the fix and ask for one clean redo
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the maths is wrong reply: 'Recompute the solution, verify it independently, and rewrite the steps with the corrected values.' If it is cluttered or unreadable: 'Reduce the stickers and tape, keep handwriting neat and large, and make sure nothing overlaps the equations.'
THE PROMPT
Create a premium vertical educational mathematics poster in a Polaroid Scrapbook Journal style, approximately 4:5 portrait.

INPUT: Read the maths problem I give you (pasted here or attached as a photo). SOLVE it yourself and verify the arithmetic by an independent check BEFORE rendering. Then put the VERIFIED solution into the poster. Problem: [PASTE THE COMPLETE PROBLEM HERE, or attach a clear photo].

FORMAT & COMPOSITION: A warm handcrafted scrapbook page - a cream kraft-paper or soft linen-textured background with gentle grain and a faint coffee-ring stain in one corner. Layer the content as a curated journal spread: white-bordered Polaroid-style frames (with the thick bottom strip), strips of pastel washi tape and torn paper, a couple of paper-clip and stitched-thread accents, and a small pressed-flower or star doodle. Portrait ~4:5. Keep generous margins and airy negative space; put ALL scrapbook decoration (tape corners, clips, doodles, little stickers) ONLY in the margins and corners, NEVER over the mathematics. Each block of work sits on its own clean taped card so the maths is crisp and uncluttered.

TYPOGRAPHY: Friendly, tidy handwritten-journal lettering in warm dark-brown and ink-blue, mixed with a couple of neat marker headers - always fully legible, never scribbly. Render all mathematical notation accurately: fractions with clear bars, factorials n!, permutations nPr (or superscript-P), combinations nCr (or binomial bracket), summation sigma, subscripts and superscripts correctly sized. Never distort numerals, symbols, or punctuation.

CONTENT TO DISPLAY (strict top-to-bottom hierarchy): (1) TITLE '[Q-number] - [short topic title]' as a bold marker header on a taped label at the top. (2) A 'Problem:' block containing the question VERBATIM, written on a Polaroid-framed card. (3) A 'Method: [precise method name]' heading on a washi-taped strip. (4) The solution as 3 to 6 concise handwritten steps; where the problem type calls for it, include a clean central SLOT/POSITION diagram (for example F _ _ R with little braces labelled 'fixed') OR a small neat rank/ledger TABLE drawn like a hand-ruled journal table. (5) A clearly boxed 'Final Answer: [value]' highlighted inside a doodled frame or a bright sticky-note.

SIGNATURE: A small, elegant handwritten signature 'Indrajeet Yadav' in a bottom corner, on a tiny tag, not overlapping any mathematics.

ACCURACY SAFEGUARDS: Copy every word and symbol exactly; verify the arithmetic independently before rendering; never drop restrictions like 'without repetition', 'distinct', 'identical', 'fixed', or 'do not come together'; show ONE method only (no alternative method); use correct permutation/combination/factorial notation; keep it readable at mobile-phone size; maintain the title -> problem -> method -> reasoning -> answer hierarchy; produce NO malformed numerals, NO duplicated or garbled digits, NO spelling mistakes, NO pseudo-handwriting gibberish, NO random extra equations, and NO decoration over the text. The result must look like a lovingly made keepsake journal page a teacher would be proud to share.
