Topographic Contour Map Solution Poster

A solved maths problem styled as an elegant topographic contour-map poster with elevation lines, ridge tints and a survey-marker answer seal.

  1. Paste the full problem (or attach a clear photo) exactly where the prompt says PASTE THE COMPLETE PROBLEM HERE

  3. Generate at the highest quality / largest portrait size offered

THE PROMPT
Create a premium vertical educational mathematics poster in the Topographic Contour Map style, approximately 4:5 portrait.

INPUT: Read the maths problem I give you (pasted here or attached as a photo). SOLVE it yourself and verify the arithmetic by an independent check BEFORE rendering. Then put the VERIFIED solution into the poster. Problem: [PASTE THE COMPLETE PROBLEM HERE, or attach a clear photo].

FORMAT & COMPOSITION: Make it look like a fine survey/elevation map. Background is warm cartographic paper - soft cream to pale sage, with faint thin concentric contour lines rippling like elevation rings, tinted in muted bands of olive, sage, sand and dusty teal. Add small map motifs ONLY in the margins and corners: a tiny compass rose, a north arrow, a scale bar, faint latitude/longitude tick marks, and a hairline neat-line border framing the page. Crucial: all contour lines and tints stay OUT of the central content column - the area holding the mathematics must read as a clean flat 'plateau' of plain paper. Portrait ~4:5, generous margins, calm balanced negative space, decorations strictly in margins/corners and NEVER over the maths.

TYPOGRAPHY: Crisp surveyor/cartographer lettering - clean condensed uppercase for headings (like map place-names) and a tidy legible serif/grotesque for body. Render mathematical notation accurately: fractions, factorials n!, permutations nPr (or superscript-P form), combinations nCr (binomial), summation, subscripts, superscripts - never distort numbers, symbols, or punctuation.

CONTENT TO DISPLAY (strict top-to-bottom hierarchy): (1) TITLE '[Q-number] - [short topic title]' set like a map title cartouche; (2) a 'Problem:' block with the question VERBATIM, framed like a survey legend box; (3) a 'Method: [precise method name]' heading; (4) the solution as 3-6 concise steps shown like elevation stations climbing toward the summit - and where the problem type needs it, include a clean central SLOT/POSITION diagram (e.g. F _ _ R with little braces labelled 'fixed') OR a small neat rank/ledger TABLE drawn as a tidy map legend in the same cartographic look; (5) a clearly boxed 'Final Answer: [value]' styled as a benchmark survey marker / triangulation seal (a small medallion stamp).

SIGNATURE: a small elegant handwritten signature 'Indrajeet Yadav' in a bottom corner, like a cartographer's hand annotation, tasteful, not overlapping any mathematics.

ACCURACY SAFEGUARDS: copy every word and symbol exactly; verify the arithmetic independently before rendering; never drop restrictions like 'without repetition', 'distinct', 'identical', 'fixed', or 'do not come together'; show ONE method only (no alternative method); use correct permutation/combination/factorial notation; keep it readable at mobile-phone size; maintain title -> problem -> method -> reasoning -> answer hierarchy; NO malformed numerals, NO duplicated or garbled digits, NO spelling mistakes, NO pseudo-handwriting gibberish, NO random extra equations, NO decoration over the text. The contour decoration must enhance, never obscure - mathematics first, map styling second.
