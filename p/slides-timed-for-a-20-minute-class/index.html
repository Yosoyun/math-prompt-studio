Slides Timed for a 20-Minute Class

A deck planned to fit a 20-minute slot, with a time budget for each slide.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and your topic.
  3. 3. Say how many minutes you have.
  4. 4. Send and check the time budget.
  5. 5. Copy slides and follow the timings.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it overruns, reply: "This is too much for [X] minutes. Cut it to fit and rebalance the timings."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who plans tightly timed lessons that finish on schedule.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I have a fixed slot of [X] minutes (default 20). I need a slide deck planned to fit that time exactly - not too much, not too little.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take my topic and the time I have.
2. Decide how many slides realistically fit, allowing time for one or two student questions.
3. Build the deck: title, slide content, and a TIME BUDGET in minutes for each slide.
4. Include a quick opening hook (about 2 minutes) and a closing recap (about 2 minutes).
5. Make the total add up to exactly the time I have and show the running total.

INPUT: Topic: [PASTE THE TOPIC HERE] / Time available: [X] minutes.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Plan the pacing internally. Use only correct, standard formulas and definitions for this board; never invent. Keep maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. Before finishing, verify two things independently: (a) the per-slide times sum exactly to the available time (show the arithmetic of the running total), and (b) every formula or example shown is correct. If the topic cannot fit the time, say so and tell me what to drop before continuing.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- A header: Topic, total time, slide count.
- For each slide: Slide [n] - [Title] - [minutes] | bullets.
- A running-total column or line so I can see the timing adds up.
- A closing line: "Total: [X] minutes (verified)."

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
- Slide 1 carries "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav"; end with footer "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: Timings must be realistic for [CLASS/GRADE]. Total must match exactly. No slide should need more than about 3 minutes. Leave a little buffer for questions. Be honest if the scope is too big.
