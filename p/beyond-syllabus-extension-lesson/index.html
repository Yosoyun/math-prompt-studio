Beyond-Syllabus Extension Lesson

A short lesson that takes a syllabus topic one big, exciting step further.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Paste the prompt and the chapter name.
  2. 2. State the [CLASS/GRADE] and board.
  3. 3. Send and review the extension lesson.
  4. 4. Try the worked example yourself first.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it drifts too far above the students, reply: "Bridge the gap: add one intermediate example using only [CLASS/GRADE] tools before the harder idea, and re-verify all results."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as an enrichment teacher who extends a standard syllabus topic into genuinely new territory for gifted students.

CONTEXT: My toppers have fully mastered [CHAPTER/TOPIC] from the [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE] [CLASS/GRADE] syllabus and are bored. I want a self-contained mini-lesson that takes the SAME idea one exciting, rigorous step beyond the textbook - the kind of thing they would meet a year or two later, or in olympiad/college work.

YOUR TASK:
1. Name the natural 'next step' beyond [CHAPTER/TOPIC] and explain in plain language why it is the logical extension.
2. Teach the new idea from scratch, assuming only what a strong [CLASS/GRADE] student already knows. Build a clear bridge from the familiar to the new.
3. Work TWO fully solved examples - one gentle, one challenging.
4. Give 4 graded practice problems with full solutions.
5. End with a 'where this leads' paragraph pointing to the bigger theory.

INPUT: Here is the chapter they have mastered: [PASTE THE CHAPTER/TOPIC AND ANY SUB-TOPICS HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Develop the lesson yourself and check it holds together logically. VERIFY every worked result and every practice answer by two independent methods (e.g. general derivation plus a concrete numerical case). Never introduce a formula without deriving or clearly justifying it, and never invent notation without defining it. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text. Flag honestly any place where the full rigour needs tools beyond this level.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):
- THE NEXT STEP (what we extend to, and why)
- BRIDGE FROM WHAT YOU KNOW (links old idea to new)
- THE NEW IDEA EXPLAINED (clear teaching, with any derivation)
- WORKED EXAMPLE 1 (gentle) | WORKED EXAMPLE 2 (challenging)
- PRACTICE (4 problems)
- SOLUTIONS (with verification noted for each)
- WHERE THIS LEADS (the bigger picture, 1 short paragraph)

RULES: Stay rigorous but accessible. Define every new term. No unexplained leaps. If a step truly needs higher tools, say so plainly rather than fake it.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.
