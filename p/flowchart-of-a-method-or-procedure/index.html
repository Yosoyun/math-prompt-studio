Flowchart of a Method or Procedure

A step-by-step decision flowchart for solving one type of problem.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Name the method or problem type.
  3. 3. Paste the prompt below.
  4. 4. Send and follow the arrows.
  5. 5. Test it on one real problem.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a step is unclear, reply: "Rewrite step [number] as a simple yes/no decision with the two paths labeled." If the logic breaks on a case, reply: "Trace your flowchart on this example and fix any step that fails: [paste example]."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who turns problem-solving methods into clear, foolproof flowcharts.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want a text FLOWCHART for one method or procedure so students always know the next step and which decision to make. No image needed - use boxes and arrows in text.

YOUR TASK:
1. Lay out the method as a numbered flow from START to END.
2. At each branching point, write a clear decision question with two labelled paths (Yes / No).
3. Include what to do in each path.
4. Add a final CHECK step where the student verifies the answer.
5. Below the flowchart, work ONE short example by walking through your own flowchart step by step.

INPUT: Here is the method or problem type I want charted: [PASTE METHOD OR PROBLEM TYPE HERE]. If I give only a name, assume the standard [BOARD] approach for [CLASS/GRADE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: First design the full logic privately and make sure every possible case has a path (no dead ends, no missing branch). VERIFY the flowchart by mentally running it on at least two different cases, including a tricky one. Never invent steps or formulas; if unsure, mark "(verify)". Keep all maths in clean plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exactly this):
METHOD: <name>
[START]
   |
   v
Step 1: <action>
   |
   v
Decision: <question>?  -- Yes --> <action / next step>
                       -- No  --> <action / next step>
(continue with steps and decisions)
   |
   v
[CHECK]: verify the answer by <independent check>
   |
   v
[END]
WORKED EXAMPLE: walk through the flowchart on a sample problem, naming each step you pass through.

RULES: Every decision must have both paths handled. No step left ambiguous. Match difficulty to [CLASS/GRADE].

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
