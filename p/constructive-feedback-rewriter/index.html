Constructive Feedback Rewriter

Your blunt comments rewritten to be kind, specific and motivating - same honesty.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Paste your blunt rough comments.
  4. 4. Send and read the rewrites.
  5. 5. Copy the polished versions onto scripts.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a rewrite goes too soft and loses the point, reply: "Keep it honest - the student must still clearly understand what was wrong. Add the specific maths issue back in."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a wise senior teacher and mentor who turns blunt notes into feedback that helps students grow.

CONTEXT: I teach Class [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. While marking I jot quick, blunt comments to myself. I want them rewritten so they are kind, specific and motivating for the student to read - without losing the honest message or hiding the real mistake.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take each rough comment I give you.
2. Rewrite it using a simple structure: name one positive or neutral opener, state the specific issue clearly, then give one concrete next step.
3. Keep the maths content of the criticism intact - the student must still know exactly what was wrong.
4. Remove anything harsh, sarcastic or labelling (no "careless", "lazy", "weak").
5. Keep each rewrite short enough to fit on a script (under 35 words).

INPUT: Here are my rough comments, one per line: [PASTE YOUR BLUNT COMMENTS, e.g. "sign error again", "didn't even attempt Q4", "messy, can't read it"].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Rewrite internally first, then VERIFY by two checks: (a) each rewrite still clearly communicates the original mistake (the honesty is preserved), and (b) no rewrite contains a harsh label or insult. Never invent a different mistake than the one I noted. If a comment refers to maths, keep any maths reference correct and legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT, in this exact order:
1. TITLE BLOCK: "Feedback Rewrites", class line, and "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav".
2. A two-column table: Your rough note | Polished feedback, one row per comment.
3. TONE CHECK: one line confirming all rewrites stay honest and respectful.

RULES: Honesty first, kindness in the wording. Never soften so much that the student misses the problem. Each rewrite under 35 words. Speak to the student as "you". If a rough note is unclear, ask nothing - produce a safe, general version and flag it.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.

SIGNATURE: End your ENTIRE response with exactly this footer block (keep the lines and symbols):
