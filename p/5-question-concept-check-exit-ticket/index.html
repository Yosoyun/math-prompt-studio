5-Question Concept Check (Exit Ticket)

A quick 5-question concept check to confirm whether today's lesson landed, with answers and a re-teach hint.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Fill class, board and today's exact topic.
  2. 2. Send the prompt.
  3. 3. Give the 5 questions in the last 5 minutes.
  4. 4. Mark with the key.
  5. 5. Re-teach the point flagged if many fail.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the questions are too broad, reply: 'Keep all 5 questions strictly on today's single concept [STATE IT], make them quick, and re-verify the answers.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher creating a fast end-of-class concept check (exit ticket).

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board]. Today I taught this exact concept: [STATE TODAY'S CONCEPT CLEARLY, e.g. 'adding fractions with unlike denominators']. I want exactly 5 quick questions to check if students understood it.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write 5 short questions strictly on today's single concept, in order of increasing demand (Q1 very easy, Q5 the deepest understanding).
2. Use a mix of one or two MCQs and short-answer items that can be done in about 5 minutes total.
3. Make Q5 a 'do they really get it' question (apply the idea, or spot why a step is wrong).

INPUT: Here is any example I used in class today: [PASTE EXAMPLE OR LEAVE BLANK].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve all 5 and VERIFY each answer a second way. Keep everything tied to today's concept only. Do not invent formulas. Keep maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
EXIT TICKET: the 5 numbered questions.
ANSWER KEY: one-line worked answer for each.
RE-TEACH SIGNAL: one line saying 'If students miss Q__, re-teach ____ tomorrow.'

RULES: Exactly 5 questions, all on today's concept, fast to complete. Difficulty suited to the class above.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block at the top.
