STEM Crossover: Link Your Maths Topic to Science

A cross-subject lesson showing your maths topic powering real science.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Type your maths topic.
  2. 2. Pick a science subject if you wish.
  3. 3. Send and review the crossover lesson.
  4. 4. Ask for more of one subject.
  5. 5. Use problems and answers in class.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a science formula looks off, reply: 'State the exact scientific formula you used, re-derive the answer, and verify it a second way.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a STEM educator who shows how a maths topic powers real science.
CONTEXT: My students are in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM]. They study science alongside maths but see the subjects as separate. I want to show my maths topic at work in physics, chemistry, or biology so they feel the connection.
YOUR TASK:
1. Name 3 concrete places this maths topic is used in school science (with the relevant scientific formula in each).
2. Create 6 cross-subject problems: 2 physics, 2 chemistry, 2 biology, each using my maths topic on a real science situation (speed-distance-time, density, concentration, magnification, growth, dosage).
3. Easy to hard.
4. Full solution for each, naming both the maths step and the science idea.
INPUT: Here is the maths topic: [PASTE TOPIC HERE].
HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve internally first. VERIFY every answer by two independent checks (recompute, and check units make sense). Use only correct, standard scientific formulas; never invent a formula or a constant. Keep units throughout and all maths legible. Fix anything that fails its check.
OUTPUT FORMAT:
- 'Maths in Science: 3 Connections' (each with the formula).
- 'Cross-Subject Problems' numbered 1 to 6, labelled by subject, with marks.
- 'Solutions' (full working with units, each ending with 'Check:').
- 'Career Hook' (1 line: a job that uses this maths-science link).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
RULES: Only class-appropriate science. Units must be correct everywhere. Simple language.
SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block at the top.
