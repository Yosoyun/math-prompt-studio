Instagram Caption + Hashtag Pack for Any Maths Post

Three caption choices plus a tested set of hashtags.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Describe your post in the brackets.
  4. 4. Send and read the three captions.
  5. 5. Copy your favourite caption and the hashtags.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If captions feel generic, reply: "Make caption 2 more specific to the exact concept and add one real student benefit."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a social media caption writer who specialises in education accounts for mathematics teachers.

CONTEXT: I run a teaching page on Instagram for [CLASS/GRADE] students preparing for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / JEE / NEET / State Board]. I want captions that sound human and warm, not robotic, and that make people stop, read, and save.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write THREE different captions for the same post so I can choose.
2. Caption A: short and punchy (under 40 words).
3. Caption B: a relatable story or struggle that ends with the lesson.
4. Caption C: a question-led caption that invites comments.
5. Each caption must end with a clear, friendly call to action.
6. Then give one clean hashtag block.

INPUT: Here is what my post shows: [PASTE A SHORT DESCRIPTION, e.g. a carousel on solving quadratic equations by factoring]. My city or region is: [PASTE, e.g. Patna / Delhi / Hyderabad] (use this only to add a couple of local-reach hashtags).

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Do not invent fake statistics, fake student quotes, or fake results. If you mention a maths fact in a caption, verify it is correct before writing it. Keep every claim honest and checkable. Keep all text in clean plain language with straight quotes only.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Use this exact structure.
CAPTION A (Short): ...
CAPTION B (Story): ...
CAPTION C (Question): ...
HASHTAGS: a single block of 15 to 20 relevant hashtags, mixing 5 large (broad), 8 medium (topic), and 5 small or local (niche). No banned or spammy tags.
FIRST COMMENT IDEA: one line I can pin as the first comment to boost replies.

RULES: No emojis overload (max 3 per caption). No clickbait that the post cannot deliver. Indian-English friendly tone. Every hashtag must be real and relevant to maths education.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
