Tricky Multi-Step Challenge (From a Photo)

A full, double-checked solution to a hard multi-step problem you photographed.

Attach a photo of the question first  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat with vision (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Take a sharp, well-lit photo of the problem.
  2. 2. Attach the photo to the chat.
  3. 3. Paste this prompt and send.
  4. 4. Read the verification before trusting the answer.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it misreads the problem, reply: "You misread the question - it actually says [CORRECT TEXT]. Re-solve from scratch and verify the final answer two ways."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a senior olympiad/JEE-Advanced solver who cracks hard multi-step problems with complete rigour and zero shortcuts.

CONTEXT: I teach gifted students at [CLASS/GRADE] preparing for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. JEE Advanced / RMO]. I will attach a photo of one tough multi-step problem. I need a flawless solution I can trust in class.

YOUR TASK:
1. First, read the photo and TYPE OUT the problem exactly as you see it, so I can confirm you read it correctly.
2. Identify the sub-problems hidden inside and outline the plan before solving.
3. Solve it fully, step by step, explaining the reasoning at each stage.
4. State the final answer clearly and boxed.
5. List the one or two steps where students most often go wrong.

INPUT: I have attached a photo of the problem. If any part of the photo is unreadable, tell me exactly which part and ask me to retype only that part - do NOT guess.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve the problem internally first, then VERIFY the final answer by at least two independent methods (e.g. an alternative algebraic route, a substitution back into the original, a numerical/limiting check, or dimensional/units check). If the two checks disagree, find your error and redo it - never present an answer you have not confirmed. Never invent formulas, values, or steps; cite any standard result by name. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):
- PROBLEM AS I READ IT (typed from the photo - confirm before trusting)
- PLAN (the sub-problems and approach)
- FULL SOLUTION (numbered steps with reasoning)
- FINAL ANSWER (clearly boxed)
- VERIFICATION (two independent checks, shown in full)
- COMMON STUDENT TRAP (1-2 lines)

RULES: No hand-waving. Every step must be justified. If the problem as written has no clean answer or seems mistyped, say so and explain. Confirm the read-out before relying on the result.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.
