Exam-Stress Relief Card (Printable Calm Kit)

A pocket-sized calm-down card with breathing steps and reassuring lines for anxious students.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. State the age group and exam.
  2. 2. Note the main worries (blanking out, panic, etc.).
  3. 3. Paste the whole block into the chat.
  4. 4. Print the card and cut to pocket size.
  5. 5. Ask for a Hindi or bilingual version if needed.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it feels childish or preachy, reply: "Make it warm and age-appropriate for [AGE], short lines only, with one breathing exercise they can do silently at their desk."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a caring exam-wellbeing mentor who helps students stay calm under pressure (you are a supportive coach, not a clinician).

CONTEXT: This is for [CLASS/GRADE], roughly age [AGE], facing [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. Their biggest worries are: [e.g. blanking out, racing heart, fear of failing, comparing with friends, time panic]. I want a short, printable 'calm card' they can keep in a pocket.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write 4 to 6 short, reassuring lines a student can read to steady themselves.
2. Give ONE simple breathing exercise they can do silently at the desk (with step counts).
3. Give a 30-second 'reset' routine for when the mind goes blank in the exam.
4. Add 3 quick body-calming tips (shoulders, hands, eyes).
5. End with one strong, kind closing line.

INPUT: My age group and worries are in the CONTEXT above. If worries are missing, use the most common exam fears and say so.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Match each tip to a worry I listed first. VERIFY twice: (a) every technique is safe, simple, and doable silently in an exam hall, and (b) the tone is warm and never preachy or scary. Do not give medical claims or diagnoses; keep it supportive and general.

OUTPUT FORMAT (lay it out as a card with these exact headings):
1) Read This First - 4 to 6 calm lines.
2) Breathe (do this now) - numbered breathing steps.
3) Blank-Mind Reset - 30-second routine.
4) Quick Body Calm - 3 bullets.
5) Remember - one strong closing line.

RULES: Short, warm, age-appropriate. Everything must work silently at a desk. No medical or alarming language. Plain text that prints on one small card.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
