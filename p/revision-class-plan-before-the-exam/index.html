Revision-Class Plan Before the Exam

A fast-paced revision lesson covering a chapter's key points and traps.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the chapter.
  3. 3. Send and review the key-points sheet.
  4. 4. Pick drills that suit your time.
  5. 5. Print and run the revision class.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it covers too much for one period, reply: "Trim to the 8 most exam-critical points and 5 quick-fire questions that fit one period."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who runs sharp, high-yield revision classes.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM] and the exam is near. I need a single revision lesson for the chapter below that recaps the must-know formulas, the question types that appear in exams, and the traps students fall into. Lesson length is [DURATION].

YOUR TASK:
1. List the must-know formulas and results for the chapter, each with its condition.
2. List the main exam question types, with one quick worked example of each.
3. Give a quick-fire drill of 8 to 10 short questions with answers.
4. List the top traps and last-minute reminders.
5. End with a 5-minute self-test and answer key.

INPUT: Here is the chapter and its sub-topics: [PASTE THE CHAPTER AND SUB-TOPICS HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve every example and drill internally first. VERIFY each final answer two independent ways and never invent formulas or values. Keep maths legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX. Keep everything tight enough to fit the stated duration.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
1. TITLE BLOCK - chapter, class, board, duration, 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2. MUST-KNOW FORMULAS - list with conditions.
3. EXAM QUESTION TYPES - each with one short worked example and two checks.
4. QUICK-FIRE DRILL - 8 to 10 questions with answers.
5. TOP TRAPS AND REMINDERS - short list.
6. 5-MINUTE SELF-TEST - questions with answer key.

RULES: Keep it high-yield and fast. Fit the duration. Every answer verified.

At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block.
