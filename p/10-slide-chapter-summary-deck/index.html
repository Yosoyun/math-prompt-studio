10-Slide Chapter Summary Deck

An entire chapter compressed into exactly 10 clear summary slides.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the chapter.
  3. 3. Send and review the 10 slides.
  4. 4. Ask to swap any slide's focus.
  5. 5. Copy into your deck.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If something important is missing, reply: "You left out [topic]. Rework the 10 slides to include it, still exactly 10."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher skilled at compressing a whole chapter into a clean summary.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I want a chapter wrap-up deck of EXACTLY 10 slides that captures everything important - ideal for the last class of a unit or quick revision.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the chapter I name.
2. Decide the 10 most important things to cover (concepts, key formulas, one or two model examples, common pitfalls, where it is used).
3. Fit them into exactly 10 slides, in a sensible order.
4. Each slide: a clear title and 3 to 5 tight bullets.
5. Slide 10 should be a one-screen 'big picture' recap of the whole chapter.

INPUT: Chapter to summarise: [PASTE THE CHAPTER NAME OR SYLLABUS POINTS HERE]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Map the full chapter internally, then ruthlessly prioritise to 10 slides. Use only correct, standard formulas and definitions for this board; never invent. Keep all maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. Before finishing, verify two things independently: (a) the 10 slides leave out nothing exam-critical for this board, and (b) every formula shown is correct (check at least the trickiest one with a quick example). Adjust the selection if a key idea is missing.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Exactly 10 slides:

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Slide 1: Chapter title + "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav".
Slides 2 to 9: Title + bullets.
Slide 10: "Big Picture Recap".
End with footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: Exactly 10 slides, no more, no fewer. No filler. Prioritise what gets tested in [BOARD or EXAM]. Keep bullets screen-friendly. If 10 truly cannot hold it, tell me and propose the smallest workable number before continuing.
