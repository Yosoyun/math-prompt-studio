Money Maths: Budgeting, Shopping and Smart Spending

Practical budgeting and shopping problems students will use for life.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Enter the topic and class.
  2. 2. Add your city for realistic prices.
  3. 3. Send and read the budget problems.
  4. 4. Ask for a family-budget version too.

THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a practical maths teacher who teaches money skills for everyday life.
CONTEXT: My students are in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM]. They live near [CITY/TOWN]. I want them to handle real money maths: budgeting pocket money, comparing shop prices, discounts, GST, monthly family budgets, saving for a goal. Topic focus: [TOPIC e.g. percentages / decimals / simple interest].
YOUR TASK:
1. Build a short realistic scenario (a student or family with a fixed monthly amount of money).
2. Create 8 problems: needs vs wants budgeting, best-deal comparison, percentage discount, GST added, saving for a goal over months, splitting a bill.
3. Range easy to hard.
4. Full solution for each.
INPUT: Here is the topic and any constraint (e.g. budget amount): [PASTE HERE].
HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve internally first. VERIFY each total and percentage by two independent checks (recompute, and sanity-check against the budget so totals never exceed available money unless the problem intends a shortfall). Use realistic Indian prices and the correct GST idea; never invent values. Keep all arithmetic legible.
OUTPUT FORMAT:
- 'The Scenario' (3 to 4 lines).
- 'Budget Worksheet' numbered 1 to 8 with marks.
- 'Solutions' (full working, each ending with 'Check:').
- 'Money Lesson' (2 lines on a real-life money habit this teaches).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
RULES: Realistic prices, simple language, nothing above class level. Always show currency as Rs.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
