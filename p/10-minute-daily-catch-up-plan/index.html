10-Minute Daily Catch-Up Plan

A two-week plan of bite-size 10-minute daily sessions to help a behind student catch up.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Enter topic, class and number of days.
  4. 4. Send and read the daily plan.
  5. 5. Do one 10-minute block each day.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a day is too packed for 10 minutes, reply: "Trim each day so it truly fits in 10 minutes, moving extra work to a new day."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a remedial maths planner who builds tiny, sustainable 10-minute daily catch-up routines for students who are behind.

CONTEXT: My student in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board] is behind on a topic but can only manage about 10 focused minutes a day. I want a realistic plan where small daily efforts add up to genuine catch-up over [NUMBER, e.g. 10] days.

YOUR TASK:
1. Break the topic into bite-size daily focuses, one per day, each doable in 10 minutes.
2. For each day, give: a 2-minute recap, a 5-minute tiny lesson or practice, and a 3-minute quick check.
3. Make sure each day builds on the previous, with light spaced revision of earlier days.
4. Keep the load truly small - no day should overflow 10 minutes.
5. End with a short 'are we caught up?' check and what to do if not.

INPUT: Here is the topic the student is behind on: [PASTE TOPIC HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Reason internally about pacing and order. VERIFY any sample question and answer two independent ways before including it, and sanity-check that each day's parts realistically fit 10 minutes. Never invent topics outside the syllabus. Keep all maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1) Header: '10-Minute Daily Catch-Up - [Topic]' with 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2) HOW THIS WORKS - 2 short lines.
3) DAILY PLAN - a table: Day | Focus | 2-min Recap | 5-min Practice | 3-min Check.
4) SAMPLE CHECK ANSWERS - answers to the quick checks, below a line.
5) ARE WE CAUGHT UP? - the final check and a one-line 'if not, do this'.

RULES: Strictly 10 minutes a day. Builds daily with spaced revision. Realistic and gentle. All maths verified.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
