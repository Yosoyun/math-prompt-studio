Full Chapter Deck with Speaker Notes on Every Slide

A complete, build-ready deck where each slide has a title, bullets, a visual cue and word-for-word teacher notes.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the whole prompt below.
  3. 3. Fill in chapter, class and slide count.
  4. 4. Send and copy slide blocks into Slides.
  5. 5. Paste notes into the Notes pane.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If notes are too long for live class, reply: 'Shorten every speaker note to about 40 words I can actually say aloud, keep the maths exact.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as an expert mathematics teacher and instructional designer who builds polished, exam-aligned lecture decks with full teacher scripts.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] mathematics for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want a complete deck I can build in PowerPoint or Google Slides with no extra work, including what to actually say on each slide.

YOUR TASK:
1. Read the chapter or topic I give you.
2. Choose a sensible slide count for one full class (default 14 to 18 unless I state a number).
3. Order the deck as a real lesson: hook, prior-knowledge recall, concept build-up, 2 to 3 worked examples, common-mistake slide, quick practice, summary, exit question.
4. For EVERY slide produce a complete block in this exact shape:
   SLIDE [n] - [short title]
   ON-SLIDE BULLETS: 2 to 4 short bullets (max 10 words each).
   VISUAL: [VISUAL: describe the diagram, graph or table needed, or write 'none'].
   SPEAKER NOTES: 60 to 90 words of word-for-word teacher talk, including one question to ask the class.
5. Mark natural pause and board-work moments inside the speaker notes.

INPUT: Here is the chapter / topic and any sub-points I must cover: [PASTE THE CHAPTER OR TOPIC HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: First plan the teaching flow silently and pick the worked examples. Solve each worked example fully yourself before writing it onto slides. Verify every final numerical answer by at least two independent checks (for example, re-derive it a second way, or substitute the answer back into the original equation) and only then put it on a slide. Never invent formulas, notation or board-specific values; use only standard results for this board. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX (for example x^2, sqrt(x), (a+b)^2, integral from 0 to 1). Before finishing, re-read the deck and check two things independently: (a) the lesson order builds with no gaps, and (b) every formula, definition and answer is correct. Fix anything wrong before showing me.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Line 1: Deck title and total slide count.
- Then one clearly separated block per slide in the exact shape above, in order.
- After the last slide: a 3-line 'Build tips' note (suggested font size, one colour for emphasis, where to add animations).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
- At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the deck title block.

RULES: Cover the whole topic with zero filler slides. Use language a [CLASS/GRADE] student understands. Keep bullets short enough to fit a real slide. Speaker notes must sound like a confident teacher talking, not like a textbook. Do not skip prerequisite ideas.

If anything is unclear, ask me one short question before you start.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
