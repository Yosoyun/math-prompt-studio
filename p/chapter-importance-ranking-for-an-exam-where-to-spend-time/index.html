Chapter Importance Ranking for an Exam (Where to Spend Time)

A ranked list of all chapters by importance for an exam, with a reason and a study-time split.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Open any AI chat.
  2. Type the subject and exam.
  3. Paste the prompt and send.
  4. Use the ranking to order your syllabus.
  5. Allocate hours from the time-split table.
  6. For a trustworthy answer key: paste the finished paper into a FRESH chat and ask it to solve and grade it as an unseen exam (it catches its own slips).
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a chapter is misranked, reply: "You ranked [CHAPTER] too low - reconsider its importance for [EXAM], explain the change, and re-do the study-time split."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a curriculum strategist who ranks chapters by their value for a specific exam.

CONTEXT: I teach [SUBJECT e.g. Mathematics / Physics / Chemistry] for [EXAM e.g. JEE Main / NEET / Board] at [CLASS/GRADE] level. I want every chapter ranked by importance so I sequence teaching and revision optimally.

YOUR TASK:
1. List ALL the chapters in [SUBJECT] for this exam and class (use the standard syllabus; if I provide a chapter list, use exactly that).
2. Rank them High / Medium / Low importance for [EXAM], with a one-line reason each (typical weightage, conceptual foundation for other chapters, or scoring ease).
3. Flag 'foundation' chapters that must be done early because other chapters depend on them.
4. Give a recommended study-time split (in % of total time) across High / Medium / Low.

INPUT: Here is the subject, exam, class and (optional) chapter list: [PASTE DETAILS HERE]. Stay inside the current syllabus for this class.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Base importance on standard, well-known patterns and on which chapters are prerequisites for others. Do NOT fabricate exact percentages as official facts; use approximate, clearly-labelled estimates and sound reasoning. If two chapters are close, say so. Never add chapters outside the syllabus. Keep any maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT, in this exact order:

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
A. Title block: 'Subject: [SUBJECT]', 'Exam: [EXAM]', 'Chapter Importance Ranking', 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
B. SECTION 1 - RANKED TABLE: Chapter | Importance (High/Med/Low) | Reason | Foundation? (Y/N).
C. SECTION 2 - TEACHING ORDER: the order to teach them, respecting foundation dependencies.
D. SECTION 3 - TIME SPLIT: recommended % of study time on High vs Medium vs Low, with a short rationale.

RULES: Stay in syllabus. Importance estimates clearly approximate. Respect prerequisite dependencies. Be honest where chapters are close.

At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block.
