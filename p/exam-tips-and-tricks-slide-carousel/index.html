Exam Tips and Tricks Slide Carousel

A punchy carousel of exam tips, time-savers and trap warnings, one idea per slide.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the chapter or exam.
  3. 3. Send and skim the tip slides.
  4. 4. Ask for more time-saving shortcuts.
  5. 5. Copy into your deck or share digitally.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a shortcut seems risky, reply: "Show me why the shortcut on slide [n] is always valid, with one example."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a top exam coach in mathematics who knows exactly where students lose marks.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I want a short, punchy carousel of exam tips - the kind students screenshot and remember - for one chapter or for the exam overall.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the chapter or exam I name.
2. Produce 8 to 12 tip slides, ONE sharp idea per slide.
3. Mix these tip types: a smart shortcut, a common trap to avoid, a time-saver, a marking-scheme tip, and a 'check your answer' habit.
4. Make each tip specific and actionable, not generic advice.
5. Give each slide a catchy short title and one or two crisp lines.

INPUT: Chapter or exam to make tips for: [PASTE HERE]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Think through where students actually lose marks in this topic. Every shortcut or formula must be correct and standard for this board - do not invent tricks that only sometimes work. Keep any maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. For each shortcut you suggest, VERIFY it two ways: test it on one concrete example, and state the condition under which it is valid. Remove any tip you cannot confirm is reliable.

OUTPUT FORMAT: A carousel, each slide as:
Slide [n] - [Tip type in brackets]: [Catchy title]
- one or two short lines
Tip types: (Shortcut), (Trap), (Time-saver), (Marks), (Check).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" on the first slide; end with footer "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: No vague advice like 'study hard'. Every tip must be usable in the exam hall. State validity conditions for shortcuts. Keep text short enough for a phone screen.
