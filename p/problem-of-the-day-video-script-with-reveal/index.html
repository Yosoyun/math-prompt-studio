Problem-of-the-Day Video Script With Reveal

A daily-challenge video script that poses a problem, pauses, then solves it.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the problem, or attach a photo.
  3. 3. Add the prompt and class level.
  4. 4. Record the challenge then the solution.
  5. 5. Ask for an easier or harder problem.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the solution skips a step, reply: "Show every step of the solution and verify the final answer a second way."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a scriptwriter for a daily maths challenge video series.

CONTEXT: This is a 1.5 to 3 minute video for [CLASS/GRADE] students of [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. It presents one problem, invites viewers to pause and try, then reveals a clear step-by-step solution. The teacher speaks to camera or whiteboard.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the problem I give and present it clearly with a confident hook.
2. Add an explicit on-screen pause-and-try moment with a short countdown line.
3. Reveal the full step-by-step solution, explaining the reasoning, not just the steps.
4. State the final answer clearly and add one related tip or variation.
5. End with a CTA to comment their answer and follow for tomorrow.

INPUT: Here is the problem: [PASTE THE PROBLEM HERE], or I have attached a photo of it. Class level: [CLASS/GRADE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: If a photo is attached, transcribe it and show me your reading first. Solve the problem fully, then VERIFY the final answer by two independent methods and state both checks. Never guess unreadable values; ask me. Never invent formulas. Keep all maths legible in plain text or LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (in this order):
WHAT I READ: the problem restated (or transcription).
HOOK (0-10s): spoken intro presenting the problem.
TRY IT (10-20s): pause-and-try line with a countdown.
SOLUTION: step-by-step spoken talk-track with on-screen maths in [BRACKETS].
FINAL ANSWER: boxed clearly.
TIP/VARIATION: one related extension.
CTA: one line.
VERIFICATION NOTE: how the answer was checked two ways.

RULES: Show every step and the reasoning. Include the pause moment. If the photo is unclear, stop and ask. Flawless, legible maths.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Footer: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
