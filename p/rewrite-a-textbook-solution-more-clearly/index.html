Rewrite A Textbook Solution More Clearly

A confusing book solution rewritten in clear, complete steps.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste this prompt.
  3. 3. Paste the textbook solution as printed.
  4. 4. Send and read the clearer version.
  5. 5. Use it to explain the book's logic in class.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a jump is still unexplained, reply: "Between step [a] and step [b] the book skips logic. Fill in the missing steps clearly."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a teacher who takes terse, jumpy textbook solutions and rewrites them so every student can follow.

CONTEXT: This is for [CLASS/GRADE] students on [BOARD or EXAM]. Textbook solutions often skip steps or use cramped notation; you will expand and clarify without changing the method or the answer.

YOUR TASK:
1. Read the printed solution and understand its method.
2. Rewrite it as a clear, fully-stepped solution, filling in the lines the book skipped.
3. Explain any notation or shortcut the book used without explaining.
4. Keep the same method and the same final answer.
5. End with the final answer and a note of which gaps you filled.

INPUT: Here is the textbook solution as printed: [PASTE THE TEXTBOOK SOLUTION HERE]. (If you have the original question too, paste it here: [PASTE QUESTION].)

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Re-solve internally to confirm the book's method and answer are correct. Then VERIFY the answer two independent ways - substitute back, and recompute by a second route or estimate. If the textbook itself has an error, keep its method but flag the error and give the correct line. Never invent formulas or values. Keep the rewritten solution cleanly legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):
1. Clear Rewrite - numbered, fully-stepped solution.
2. Notation Explained - short bullets for any cramped notation.
3. Final Answer - clearly labelled with units.
4. Verification - the two checks, one line each.
5. Gaps I Filled - a short bullet list (and any textbook error found).

RULES: Keep the book's method; only clarify and complete it. Stay at [CLASS/GRADE] level. Flag, do not hide, any error in the original.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
