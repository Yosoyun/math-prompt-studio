8-Slide Instagram Carousel That Teaches One Concept

Ready-to-type text for 8 carousel slides plus a caption.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat in your browser.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Replace the bracket parts with your topic and class.
  4. 4. Press send and wait for 8 slides.
  5. 5. Copy each slide into Canva or PowerPoint.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a slide is too crowded, reply: "Slide 4 has too many words. Rewrite it in under 20 words and move the rest to a new slide."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as an expert mathematics teacher and a social media content writer who creates clean, high-engagement Instagram carousels.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE e.g. Class 11] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. My audience is students and parents who follow me for clear maths help. I will turn your text into 8 carousel slides using Canva, so each slide must hold a small, readable amount of text.

YOUR TASK:
1. Build exactly 8 slides that teach ONE concept clearly from start to finish.
2. Slide 1 is a scroll-stopping hook (a bold promise or a surprising line).
3. Slides 2 to 7 teach the idea in tiny, logical steps with one small worked example.
4. Slide 8 is a call to action (save, share, follow, or comment).
5. Keep every slide skimmable: short lines, no paragraph longer than 2 lines.

INPUT: Here is the concept I want to teach: [PASTE THE CONCEPT OR QUESTION HERE, e.g. how to find the domain of a function].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve any maths internally first. Before you write the slides, VERIFY your worked example by two independent checks (for example, plug the answer back in AND test one edge value). Never invent formulas or values. Write all maths in clean, simple plain text that reads correctly on a phone (use ^ for powers, sqrt() for roots, and / for fractions). If a step is risky, double-check it before showing it.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Use this exact structure.
SLIDE 1 (Hook): ...
SLIDE 2: ...
SLIDE 3: ...
SLIDE 4: ...
SLIDE 5: ...
SLIDE 6: ...
SLIDE 7: ...
SLIDE 8 (Call to action): ...
DESIGN NOTES: one short line per slide telling me what to make big or bold.
CAPTION: a 3 to 5 line caption to post with the carousel.
VERIFICATION: 2 lines proving the worked example is correct.

RULES: Maximum 30 words per slide. No jargon a parent cannot read. Friendly, confident tone. The maths must be 100 percent correct. Do not use any symbols that look broken in plain text.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
