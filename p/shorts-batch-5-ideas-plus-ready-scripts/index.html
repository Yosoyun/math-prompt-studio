Shorts Batch: 5 Ideas Plus Ready Scripts

Five distinct short ideas with full ready-to-record scripts in one go.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and your topic area.
  3. 3. State class level.
  4. 4. Film all five in one session.
  5. 5. Ask to swap any idea you dislike.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If two ideas feel similar, reply: "Replace idea [number] with a clearly different angle on the same topic."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a batch content producer who plans a week of maths shorts at once.

CONTEXT: A teacher wants to film 5 different shorts in one sitting for [CLASS/GRADE] students of [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET], all from one topic area but each a clearly different angle. Each short is 30 to 45 seconds, spoken to camera.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the topic area I give and generate 5 distinct short ideas (e.g. a trick, a mistake, a fast method, a why-question, a one-formula explainer).
2. For each idea write a full ready-to-record script: hook, body with one worked example, payoff, CTA.
3. Make sure no two scripts overlap in idea or example.
4. Add 3 on-screen caption phrases per short.

INPUT: Here is the topic area and level: [PASTE TOPIC AREA AND CLASS LEVEL HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve every example fully and VERIFY each final answer by two independent methods. Never reuse the same example twice. Never invent formulas; use correct standard results. Keep all maths legible in plain text or LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (in this order):
BATCH TITLE: one line.
For each short (Short 1 to 5): ANGLE (one word) | HOOK (spoken) | BODY (spoken + on-screen maths in [BRACKETS]) | FINAL ANSWER | CTA | 3 ON-SCREEN CAPTIONS.
VERIFICATION NOTE: one line per short confirming the two checks.

RULES: 5 genuinely different shorts. Each under 110 spoken words. No repeated examples. Flawless, legible maths.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Footer: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
