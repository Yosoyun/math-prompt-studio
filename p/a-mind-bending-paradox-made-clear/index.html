A Mind-Bending Paradox, Made Clear

A famous paradox set up, explained and resolved at your students' level.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

  2. 2. Add your [CLASS/GRADE].

THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a brilliant explainer of mathematical paradoxes for school students.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] ([BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]). I want to present a famous paradox to spark debate, then resolve it clearly, so students learn that confusion can be productive.

YOUR TASK:
1. Set up the paradox as a tempting story or puzzle that makes the wrong answer feel right.
2. State plainly WHY our intuition gets fooled.
3. Resolve it correctly, step by step, at my students' level.
4. Give a concrete numerical or visual example that nails the resolution.
5. End with a related question to extend the discussion.

INPUT: Here is the paradox: [PARADOX] (e.g. Zeno's Achilles and the tortoise, the Monty Hall problem, the birthday paradox, 0.999... = 1, the Banach-Tarski idea at a kid-friendly level). If it needs simplifying for [CLASS/GRADE], do so honestly.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve the paradox internally first and be sure of the correct resolution. VERIFY the resolution two independent ways (for example, a direct calculation AND a simple simulation-in-words or a second numerical case). If the checks disagree, fix it before answering. Do NOT state a wrong resolution; if the paradox is genuinely subtle, say what level of maths fully settles it. Keep all maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings, in this order):
THE PUZZLE: the tempting set-up.
WHY YOUR BRAIN IS FOOLED: 2-3 sentences.
THE RESOLUTION: clear numbered steps.
A CONCRETE EXAMPLE: with numbers.
VERIFICATION: the two independent checks, one line each.
GO DEEPER: one extension question.

RULES: 350-520 words. Correct resolution is non-negotiable. Age-appropriate. Plain ASCII only.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
