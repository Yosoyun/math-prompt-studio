Explain It at 5 Levels: Child to Expert

The same concept explained five ways, from a 6-year-old to an expert.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Enter your concept name.
  4. 4. Send and read all five levels.
  5. 5. Use the level closest to your class.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a level is mislabelled, reply: "Level 3 is too advanced for a school student. Make it match a Class 9 learner and keep the others as they are."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who can explain any idea at five different depths.

CONTEXT: I teach across levels for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want this concept explained at five increasing levels so I can pitch it to any audience: [WRITE THE CONCEPT HERE].

YOUR TASK:
1. Explain the SAME concept five times, each deeper than the last.
2. Level 1: to a curious 6-year-old (only everyday words).
3. Level 2: to a Class 6 to 8 school student.
4. Level 3: to a Class 9 to 12 student (introduce the real notation).
5. Level 4: to a college fresher or exam topper (full rigour-lite).
6. Level 5: to a fellow expert teacher (precise, with conditions and subtleties).
7. After all five, note the ONE idea that survives at every level.

INPUT: Here is the concept: [PASTE OR TYPE THE CONCEPT HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Keep the core idea identical across levels; only the depth and vocabulary change. VERIFY that nothing said at a lower level is contradicted at a higher level; if there is a simplification, flag it honestly at the higher level. Never invent formulas or false claims. Keep any maths legible in clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
- CONCEPT
- LEVEL 1 - AGE 6
- LEVEL 2 - MIDDLE SCHOOL
- LEVEL 3 - SENIOR SCHOOL (with correct notation)
- LEVEL 4 - COLLEGE FRESHER
- LEVEL 5 - EXPERT TEACHER (conditions and subtleties)
- THE THREAD THAT NEVER CHANGES (1 to 2 lines)

RULES: Each level must be self-contained and honest. No level may teach something a later level has to fully retract; mark simplifications clearly. Keep Level 1 free of all jargon.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
