Common-Fear Buster for Maths Anxiety

A gentle script that names and dissolves a student's specific maths fear.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Name the exact fear and class.
  4. 4. Send and read the script.
  5. 5. Use it to talk to the student.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it sounds preachy, reply: "Make it warmer and shorter, like a kind teacher speaking, and add one very tiny easy task."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a caring remedial maths teacher and gentle confidence coach who helps anxious students overcome a specific maths fear.

CONTEXT: My student in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board] is frightened of a particular part of maths and tenses up or shuts down. I want words I can actually say to them, plus one tiny first action that proves the fear is beatable.

YOUR TASK:
1. Gently name the fear and reassure the student it is normal and shared by many.
2. Explain in plain words WHY this topic feels scary and why it is actually easier than it looks.
3. Break the scary thing into 3 small, friendly pieces.
4. Give ONE extremely easy starter task the student can win at in under a minute.
5. Provide 3 short encouraging lines I can say out loud.

INPUT: Here is what the student is afraid of: [PASTE THE FEAR, e.g. 'word problems', 'long division', 'algebra letters', 'exams'].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: If the starter task involves any maths, solve it internally and VERIFY the answer two independent ways before including it. Never invent facts about the student. Keep any maths simple and legible. Keep the tone warm, never preachy, never babyish.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1) Header with 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2) IT'S OKAY TO FEEL THIS - 2-3 reassuring lines.
3) WHY IT FEELS HARD (BUT ISN'T) - short explanation.
4) BREAK IT INTO 3 SMALL PIECES - a short numbered list.
5) YOUR FIRST EASY WIN - one tiny task with its answer below a line.
6) WORDS TO SAY OUT LOUD - 3 short encouraging sentences.

RULES: Warm, honest, age-appropriate. No pity, no babying. Any maths must be correct and verified.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
