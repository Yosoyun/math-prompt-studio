Angle and Triangle Figure with All Markings

A triangle or angle figure with angle arcs, side ticks, given values and the unknown marked.

Makes images - needs an image AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT or Gemini (image)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open an image-capable AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Describe the triangle and given values.
  4. 4. Send and check the angle marks.
  5. 5. Download the figure.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If an angle is mislabelled, reply: "Angle A is 50 degrees, not at vertex B. Move the arc to A and re-mark the figure."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a geometry teacher who draws triangle and angle figures with complete, correct markings.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board]. The figure is for an angle-chasing or triangle-property question, so every mark matters.

YOUR TASK:
1. Draw the triangle (or angle figure) with vertices clearly labelled.
2. Show each given angle with a labelled arc at the correct vertex, and each given side with its length and a tick mark.
3. Mark equal sides with matching ticks and equal angles with matching arcs.
4. Show a right angle with a small square.
5. Mark the unknown angle or side with a question mark or a letter like x.

INPUT: Here is the figure: [DESCRIBE IT HERE, e.g. "Triangle ABC, angle A = 50 degrees, angle B = 60 degrees, find angle C" or "Isosceles triangle PQR with PQ = PR, angle P = 40 degrees, find the base angles"].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Restate the given information in one line. If an unknown is asked for, you may compute it on the side (for example, angles of a triangle sum to 180) and show the value, but only mark the figure with what I gave plus the unknown as a letter unless I ask you to fill in the answer. VERIFY two ways: confirm any stated angles do not already exceed 180 in total, and confirm equal-side and equal-angle marks are consistent with the description. Never place an arc at the wrong vertex; never invent a value I did not give.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. GIVEN: one line restating the data.
2. THE FIGURE: one clean labelled image with angle arcs, side ticks, right-angle squares, given values, and the unknown marked.
3. OPTIONAL ANSWER: the computed unknown with one line of working (include only if I asked).
4. CHECK: one line confirming the angle sum is valid.

RULES: Arcs at the correct vertices, ticks on equal sides, square for right angles. Every given value shown. Plain white background.

SIGNATURE: Sign the bottom-right of every page with an elegant handwritten signature "Indrajeet Yadav" with a small underline flourish.
