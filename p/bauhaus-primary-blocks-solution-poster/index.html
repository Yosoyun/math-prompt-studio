Bauhaus Primary Blocks Solution Poster

A bold geometric poster of red-yellow-blue blocks, black grid lines and circles in classic Bauhaus style.

Makes images - needs an image AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT Plus (image) or Gemini

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Paste the full problem text or attach a clear photo
  2. Ask it to solve and verify the arithmetic before composing
  3. Request 4:5 portrait at the highest image quality
  4. Make sure colour blocks sit behind decoration, not over equations
  5. Reply 'one more, bigger type' if anything is hard to read
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the maths is wrong, reply: 'Incorrect - recompute, verify the final value independently, then regenerate.' If cluttered, reply: 'Too noisy - keep the maths on clean white panels, push the colour blocks and shapes to the margins, and increase text size.'
THE PROMPT
Create a premium vertical educational mathematics poster in classic Bauhaus primary-blocks style, approximately 4:5 portrait.

INPUT: Read the maths problem I give you (pasted here or attached as a photo). SOLVE it yourself and verify the arithmetic by an independent check BEFORE rendering. Then put the VERIFIED solution into the poster. Problem: [PASTE THE COMPLETE PROBLEM HERE, or attach a clear photo].

FORMAT & COMPOSITION: Background is clean warm off-white with a crisp modular grid. Bold flat geometric blocks in the strict Bauhaus palette - primary red, primary yellow, cobalt blue, plus solid black and white - arranged as rectangles, perfect circles, half-circles and triangles with confident thick black rules between them. Slight matte paper grain for an authentic vintage print feel. Generous margins, deliberate asymmetric-but-balanced composition, lots of breathing room. Decoration - the colour blocks, a big primary circle, a black diagonal bar - lives ONLY in the margins, header band and corners; the mathematics always sits on clean white or pale panels, NEVER on top of busy colour. Mood: confident, modernist, playful-yet-rigorous, form-follows-function.

TYPOGRAPHY: A geometric sans-serif throughout (even, circular, Bauhaus-style lettering); title can be oversized and lowercase as a design accent; the mathematics in clean black sans, perfectly legible. Render notation accurately - fractions with crisp horizontal bars, factorials as n!, permutations as nPr (or superscript P), combinations as nCr (binomial), summation sigma, subscripts and superscripts correctly placed. Never distort numbers, symbols, or punctuation.

CONTENT (strict top-to-bottom hierarchy): (1) TITLE in bold geometric sans: '[Q-number] - [short topic title]', optionally set inside a primary-colour header band. (2) A 'Problem:' block with the question VERBATIM on a white panel with a thick black outline. (3) A 'Method: [precise method name]' heading. (4) The solution in 3-6 concise steps; where the type needs it, include a clean central slot/position diagram (e.g. F _ _ R with little black braces labelled 'fixed') OR a small neat rank/ledger table built from thick black gridlines with primary-colour header cells. (5) A clearly boxed 'Final Answer: [value]' inside a bold red or blue rectangle with white text.

SIGNATURE: a small, elegant handwritten signature 'Indrajeet Yadav' in the bottom corner, tasteful, not overlapping any mathematics.

ACCURACY SAFEGUARDS: copy every word and symbol exactly; verify the arithmetic independently before rendering; never drop restrictions like 'without repetition', 'distinct', 'identical', 'fixed', or 'do not come together'; show ONE method only (no alternative method); use correct permutation, combination and factorial notation; keep it readable at mobile-phone size; maintain title -> problem -> method -> reasoning -> answer hierarchy; NO malformed numerals, NO duplicated or garbled digits, NO spelling mistakes, NO pseudo-handwriting gibberish, NO random extra equations, NO decoration over the text.
