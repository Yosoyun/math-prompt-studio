Research-Style Investigation Project

An open-ended investigation with guiding questions, like a mini research project.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Paste the prompt and a seed idea.
  2. 2. Set the [CLASS/GRADE] level.
  3. 3. Send and read the staged plan.
  4. 4. Hide the 'expected findings' section from students.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the stages jump ahead too fast, reply: "Slow the early stages: add more small, concrete data-gathering steps before any conjecture, and verify the patterns you claim."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics research mentor who designs open-ended investigations that let gifted students behave like real mathematicians.

CONTEXT: I want a multi-day investigation project for strong students in [CLASS/GRADE] (target level [BOARD or EXAM e.g. RMO / college foundation]). They should gather data, spot patterns, form conjectures, test them, and try to justify them - not just answer a question.

YOUR TASK:
1. Turn the seed idea I give into a rich, open-ended investigation with a clear central question.
2. Break it into 4 stages: (A) Explore and collect data, (B) Notice patterns, (C) Make and test conjectures, (D) Prove or partially justify.
3. For each stage give concrete prompts/tasks the student does themselves, plus 'teacher notes' on what to look for.
4. Provide an 'Expected Findings' section (for the teacher only) with the patterns, the likely conjecture, and at least one proof or proof sketch.
5. Suggest 2 extension directions for students who finish early.

INPUT: Here is the seed idea / starting pattern: [PASTE THE SEED IDEA HERE]. If I leave it blank, choose a rich seed suitable for the level.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Carry out the investigation yourself first so your guidance is real, not guessed. VERIFY every claimed pattern and result by two independent checks (e.g. compute several more cases AND give a reason it must hold). Never present a conjecture as proven unless you actually prove it; clearly separate 'observed', 'conjectured', and 'proved'. Never invent data. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):
- CENTRAL QUESTION
- WHY IT'S WORTH INVESTIGATING (2 lines)
- STAGE A: EXPLORE (student tasks + teacher notes)
- STAGE B: NOTICE PATTERNS (student tasks + teacher notes)
- STAGE C: CONJECTURE & TEST (student tasks + teacher notes)
- STAGE D: PROVE OR JUSTIFY (student tasks + teacher notes)
- EXPECTED FINDINGS - TEACHER ONLY (patterns, conjecture, proof/sketch, with verification shown)
- EXTENSIONS (2 directions)

RULES: Keep it genuinely open-ended - no single forced path. Be honest about what is provable at this level. Separate observation from proof clearly.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.
