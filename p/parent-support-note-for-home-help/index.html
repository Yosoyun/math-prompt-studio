Parent-Support Note for Home Help

A warm, jargon-free note guiding parents to support a struggling child at home.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Enter the topic and the child's difficulty.
  4. 4. Send and read the note.
  5. 5. Send it home with the student.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it is too long or technical, reply: "Shorten it, remove all maths jargon, and write it for a parent who never liked maths."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a caring class teacher writing a short, warm note to help parents support a struggling maths student at home.

CONTEXT: A student in [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board] needs extra help at home, but the parents may not be confident in maths themselves and may not know technical terms. I want a friendly note they can actually understand and act on.

YOUR TASK:
1. Open with a warm, reassuring line that the child is capable and improving.
2. Explain in plain, jargon-free words what the child is currently working on and where they need support.
3. Give 3-4 simple things the parent can do at home in 10-15 minutes a day (no special knowledge needed).
4. Give 2 short 'what to say' phrases that encourage rather than pressure the child.
5. Add one gentle 'please avoid' line (e.g. avoid comparing with siblings).
6. Close with an invitation to reach out to me.

INPUT: Here is the topic and the child's difficulty: [PASTE TOPIC AND DIFFICULTY HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: If you include any sample home activity with numbers, VERIFY it two independent ways first. Never invent facts about the child or family. Keep language at the level of a parent who disliked maths in school. Avoid all jargon.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1) Title/header block including 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2) Dear Parent, - the warm opening line.
3) WHAT YOUR CHILD IS LEARNING - plain explanation.
4) HOW YOU CAN HELP AT HOME - 3-4 simple steps.
5) KIND WORDS TO USE - 2 phrases.
6) PLEASE AVOID - one gentle line.
7) Warm closing and invitation to contact the teacher.

RULES: Warm, simple, jargon-free. Practical for non-maths parents. Any maths verified. Respectful, never blaming.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
