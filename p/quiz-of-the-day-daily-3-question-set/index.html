Quiz of the Day (Daily 3-Question Set)

A short daily quiz - one recall, one application, one challenge - with answers, ready to post each day.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Fill class, board and today's chapter.
  2. 2. Send the prompt.
  3. 3. Post the 3 questions on the board or group.
  4. 4. Reveal answers later from the key.
  5. 5. Ask again tomorrow for a new set.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If two questions feel the same, reply: 'Make the three questions clearly different - one recall, one application, one challenge - and re-verify the answers.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who posts a short 'Quiz of the Day' to keep students in daily practice.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. Today's chapter or theme is [CHAPTER/THEME]. I want a clean 3-question daily quiz.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write exactly 3 questions on today's chapter: Q1 = quick RECALL (a formula, term or fact), Q2 = APPLICATION (one typical problem), Q3 = CHALLENGE (a slightly harder or thought-provoking one).
2. Keep it short enough to attempt in about 5 to 7 minutes.
3. Use whatever format fits (MCQ or short-answer) and label each.
4. Add a friendly one-line motivational header for the day.

INPUT: Here is any specific sub-topic for today: [PASTE TOPIC OR LEAVE BLANK].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve all three and VERIFY each answer a second way. Ensure the three differ in type and difficulty. Do not invent formulas. Keep maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
HEADER: 'Quiz of the Day - [date or chapter]' with a one-line nudge.
QUESTIONS: Q1 (Recall), Q2 (Application), Q3 (Challenge).
ANSWER KEY (post later): one-line answer for each, with a tiny hint for Q3.

RULES: Exactly 3 questions, three different types, short and self-contained. Difficulty suited to the class above.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block at the top.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.

SIGNATURE: End your ENTIRE response with exactly this footer block (keep the lines and symbols):
