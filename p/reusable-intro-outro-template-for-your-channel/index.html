Reusable Intro + Outro Template for Your Channel

A branded intro and outro script you can reuse on every video.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt with your channel name.
  3. 3. Pick the tone you like.
  4. 4. Save the template to reuse weekly.
  5. 5. Ask for short and long versions.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it sounds generic, reply: "Make it warmer and more specific to maths students, and keep the intro under 12 seconds."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a YouTube channel branding writer for a mathematics educator.

CONTEXT: A maths teacher wants a reusable intro and outro they can speak at the start and end of every video for [CLASS/GRADE] students of [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. It must be short, warm, and have blanks to fill per video.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write a 8 to 12 second spoken INTRO that greets viewers, states the channel promise, and has a blank for today's topic.
2. Write a 12 to 18 second spoken OUTRO that recaps, asks for subscribe/like, and points to the next video.
3. Provide each in two tones: friendly and energetic.
4. Include [BLANKS] the teacher fills in each time (topic, next video, etc.).
5. Add 3 optional one-line transition phrases to move between sections mid-video.

INPUT: Here is my channel name, subject focus and level: [PASTE CHANNEL NAME, FOCUS AND CLASS LEVEL HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Keep wording natural to read aloud. Do not invent claims (subscriber counts, awards) that may be untrue. Keep any maths references generic so the template fits all videos. No verification of maths is needed here since there are no calculations; instead double-check that every blank is clearly marked.

OUTPUT FORMAT (in this order):
INTRO - FRIENDLY: spoken lines with [BLANKS].
INTRO - ENERGETIC: spoken lines with [BLANKS].
OUTRO - FRIENDLY: spoken lines with [BLANKS].
OUTRO - ENERGETIC: spoken lines with [BLANKS].
TRANSITIONS: 3 one-line phrases.
USAGE NOTE: one line on how to fill the blanks each time.

RULES: Intros and outros must hit the stated time. All blanks clearly bracketed. No false claims. Sounds human, not corporate.


MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Footer: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
