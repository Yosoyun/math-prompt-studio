Labelled Geometry Figure, Drawn Right

A clean, correctly labelled geometry figure on a white page, ready to project or print.

Makes images - needs an image AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT or Gemini (image)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open an image-capable AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Type your exact figure in the INPUT line.
  4. 4. Send and wait for the picture.
  5. 5. Download or screenshot the result.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a label sits in the wrong spot, reply: "Move label B to the top vertex and keep all sides straight; redraw the same figure."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a precise geometry draughtsman who produces clean, exam-style labelled figures.

CONTEXT: I am a mathematics teacher preparing a figure for [CLASS/GRADE] following [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE]. The figure must look like a textbook diagram: simple, accurate, fully labelled, no clutter.

YOUR TASK:
1. Read my figure description carefully.
2. Draw the figure exactly as described, in 2D, flat-on (no fake 3D shading).
3. Label every point, side, angle and given measurement I mention.
4. Mark equal sides with tick marks and equal angles with matching arcs.
5. Show right angles with a small square, not a curved arc.
6. Keep the background plain white with thin black lines.

INPUT: Here is the figure: [DESCRIBE THE FIGURE HERE, e.g. "Triangle ABC, right-angled at B, AB = 6 cm, BC = 8 cm, with D the midpoint of AC; join BD"].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Before drawing, restate my description in your own words in one short paragraph so I can confirm you understood. Internally check that the relationships I gave are consistent (for example, that a stated right angle and stated side lengths do not contradict each other). If anything is impossible or unclear, tell me in one line and ask before drawing. Never invent measurements I did not give; if a length is unknown, leave it as a letter. Keep all text large enough to read when projected.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. CONFIRMATION: one short paragraph restating the figure.
2. THE FIGURE: one clean labelled image on a white page.
3. LABEL KEY: a short list naming each labelled point and what it is (only if helpful).

RULES: Lines must be straight and meet exactly at the named points. No decorative colours, no shadows, no watermark text of your own. Every given value must appear on the figure. Do not crop any label.

SIGNATURE: Sign the bottom-right of every page with an elegant handwritten signature "Indrajeet Yadav" with a small underline flourish.
