Deck with a Strong Hook Opening

Three opening-hook options plus a deck that pays the hook off by the end.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and your topic.
  3. 3. Send and read the three hook ideas.
  4. 4. Tell it which hook you like.
  5. 5. Use the finished deck it returns.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the hook feels flat, reply: "Give 3 fresh hooks that are more surprising and tied directly to [topic]."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a charismatic mathematics teacher who opens every class with a hook nobody forgets.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I want a lecture deck that opens with a powerful hook - a surprising fact, a puzzle, a real-world story, or a 'how is this even possible' question - and then teaches the topic so the hook is resolved by the end.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take my topic.
2. First propose THREE different hook openings (each one short, surprising, and directly tied to the topic).
3. Then build a full deck (about 12 to 16 slides) using a default hook, but clearly marked so I can swap in another.
4. Structure: Hook -> why it matters -> teach the concept -> worked example -> resolve the hook -> recap.
5. End by explicitly answering the opening hook so students feel the payoff.

INPUT: Topic for the lecture: [PASTE THE TOPIC HERE]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Brainstorm hooks internally and keep the best three. Any fact or figure used in a hook must be true and any maths must be correct and standard for this board - never invent statistics or results. Keep maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. Before finishing, verify two things separately: (a) the hook is factually/mathematically accurate, and (b) the deck actually resolves the hook with correct reasoning (check the key calculation with real numbers). Fix anything that does not hold up.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Section A: "Three hook options" (Hook 1, 2, 3, each 1 to 2 lines).
- Section B: "Full deck" - Slide [n]: [Title] + bullets, with the chosen hook on Slide 1 and the payoff clearly on the resolving slide.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
- Slide 1 carries "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav"; end with footer "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: Hooks must be genuinely surprising, not clichés. The payoff must clearly answer the hook. Keep slides short. Match tone and difficulty to [CLASS/GRADE].

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
