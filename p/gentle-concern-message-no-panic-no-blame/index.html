Gentle Concern Message (No Panic, No Blame)

A tactful note raising a worry without alarming the parent or child.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt; describe the concern.
  3. 3. Choose your tone in the brackets.
  4. 4. Read carefully before sending.
  5. 5. Ask it to soften further if needed.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it feels too soft or too strong, reply: "Make the tone [gentler / a little firmer] and keep it under 140 words."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a caring, diplomatic maths teacher who must raise a concern with a parent without causing alarm or blame.

CONTEXT: My student [STUDENT NAME], in [CLASS/GRADE], studying [SUBJECT] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET], has shown a problem I need to mention to the parent. I want the message to feel supportive and solution-focused, never accusatory, so the parent becomes a partner, not defensive.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write one short, kind message that states the concern clearly but gently.
2. Frame it as something we can fix together, not a complaint.
3. Offer 1 to 2 concrete, easy steps for home and 1 thing I will do at school.
4. Invite the parent to reply or talk, with a calm, open tone.

INPUT - the real situation (use only these facts):
- The concern: [e.g. incomplete homework / falling marks / not asking doubts / distraction]
- When/how often I noticed it: [DETAILS]
- Any positive I can still mention: [STRENGTH, optional]
- Tone I want: [very gentle / gentle but clear]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Use only the facts above; do not invent incidents, marks, or motives. Avoid words that judge the child's character (lazy, careless, weak); describe behaviour and patterns instead. Re-read once and remove anything that could read as blame or threat.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
SUBJECT LINE: calm and neutral.
MESSAGE: 110 to 150 words, warm, in short paragraphs, ready for email or WhatsApp.
TWO ALTERNATIVE OPENING LINES: in case the first feels too soft or too direct.

RULES:
- Never alarm; never blame the child or parent.
- Always pair the concern with a way forward.
- Plain, clean text only.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
