Confidence-Building Daily Routine (Exam Week)

A simple daily routine for exam week that builds steady confidence and calm.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Note your students' confidence level now.
  2. 2. State the exam-week daily schedule.
  3. 3. Paste the whole block into the chat.
  4. 4. Share the routine with students.
  5. 5. Ask for a printable morning + night checklist.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it feels heavy or vague, reply: "Make it light and doable in a busy exam week - small daily wins, sleep and food included, with a 2-minute morning confidence ritual."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a supportive performance coach who builds students' confidence through small, steady daily habits.

CONTEXT: This is for [CLASS/GRADE] during exam week for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. Right now their confidence is [e.g. shaky, up-and-down, low after a tough paper, decent but jittery]. A typical exam-week day looks like [e.g. exam in the morning, study in the afternoon, sleep by 10pm].

YOUR TASK:
1. Design a light daily routine for exam week that builds confidence without exhaustion.
2. Include a 2-minute morning confidence ritual (posture, one affirming-but-honest line, quick easy-win warm-up problem).
3. Include healthy basics: sleep target, meals, short movement, screen limits.
4. Add an 'after the exam' habit so a bad paper does not crush the next day (no post-mortems with friends, reset, look forward).
5. Add a short night routine that ends the day calmly and preps the next exam.

INPUT: My students' confidence level and daily schedule are in the CONTEXT above. If missing, assume a jittery class with morning exams and say so.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Decide the day's flow (morning -> exam -> after -> evening -> night) first. VERIFY twice: (a) the routine is genuinely light and doable in a busy exam week, and (b) every habit supports confidence or calm, nothing adds pressure. Keep it honest - no empty hype, no fake affirmations.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
1) The Idea In 2 Lines.
2) Morning Confidence Ritual - 2-minute steps.
3) During The Day - study + healthy basics.
4) After Each Exam - reset routine, 3 steps.
5) Night Wind-Down - 3 calming steps.
6) Daily Tick-Box Card - a short morning + night checklist.

RULES: Light, doable, honest. Include sleep and food. No fake hype. Plain language students will actually follow during a stressful week.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
