Flipped-Classroom Lesson Plan

A before-class study guide plus an in-class problem-solving plan.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and your topic.
  3. 3. Send and review both parts.
  4. 4. Share the home part with students.
  5. 5. Teach from the in-class part.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the home task is too long, reply: "Cut the at-home study to under 15 minutes of reading plus 3 questions, and move the rest into class."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who runs successful flipped classrooms.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. In a flipped lesson, students learn the basics at home, then we solve problems together in class. Class time is [DURATION]. Many of my students have limited internet, so the home part must work as printed reading, not only video.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write a BEFORE-CLASS study guide students can read at home in about 15 minutes: key idea, one or two worked examples, and 3 short check questions with answers.
2. Write an IN-CLASS plan that assumes the basics are known: a quick recap check, then graded group problem-solving, then a stretch challenge.
3. Give a 3-question entry quiz to confirm students did the home part, with answers.
4. Provide full solutions for all in-class problems.
5. Add a fallback mini-explanation for students who did not prepare.

INPUT: Here is the topic: [PASTE THE TOPIC HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve all examples and problems internally first. VERIFY each final answer two independent ways and never invent values or formulas. Keep maths legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX. Make sure the home part is genuinely shorter than the in-class part.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
1. TITLE BLOCK - topic, class, board, 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2. BEFORE-CLASS STUDY GUIDE - key idea, worked examples, 3 check questions with answers.
3. ENTRY QUIZ - 3 questions with answers.
4. IN-CLASS PLAN - timed recap, group problems, challenge, all with full solutions and two checks each.
5. FALLBACK FOR UNPREPARED STUDENTS - a short catch-up explanation.
6. WHAT TO COLLECT - what students hand in at the end.

RULES: Home part must work without internet. In-class part must be the bulk of the learning. Every answer verified.

At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block.
