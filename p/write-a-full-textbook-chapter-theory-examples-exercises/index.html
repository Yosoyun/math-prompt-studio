Write a Full Textbook Chapter (Theory + Examples + Exercises)

One complete, ready-to-print chapter with theory, solved examples and graded exercises.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a senior mathematics textbook author and examiner.

CONTEXT: I am a mathematics teacher preparing a printed textbook chapter for [CLASS/GRADE] students following [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. The chapter topic is [TOPIC e.g. Quadratic Equations]. Students are [average / mixed / advanced]. The chapter must read like a polished published textbook, not chat notes.

YOUR TASK:
1. Write a complete self-contained chapter on the topic.
2. Open with a 2-3 line introduction connecting the topic to earlier ideas and to real life.
3. Build the theory in a logical order, defining every new term the first time it appears.
4. State each formula clearly and show where it comes from in one or two lines.
5. Give 6 to 8 fully solved examples, easy to hard, each with a short reason for every step.
6. End with a graded exercise: 8 basic, 6 moderate, 4 challenging questions.

INPUT: Here is the exact scope to cover: [PASTE SYLLABUS POINTS OR LEAVE BLANK TO USE THE STANDARD SYLLABUS FOR THIS TOPIC].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve every example fully on your own first. Before printing each final answer, VERIFY it by at least two independent checks (for example substitute back into the original equation AND check by a different method or estimation). Never invent a formula, value, or rule; if something is outside the standard syllabus, leave it out. Keep all mathematics fully legible using clean LaTeX or clear plain text, with one step per line.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings in this order):
1. Chapter Title
2. Learning Outcomes (4-6 bullet points)
3. Introduction
4. Theory and Concepts (with sub-headings)
5. Key Formulas (boxed list)
6. Solved Examples (numbered, with steps)
7. Exercise (Basic / Moderate / Challenging, numbered)
8. Answers to Exercise (final answers only)

RULES: Use correct notation throughout. No step may be skipped in solved examples. Difficulty must rise gradually. Do not include any question you cannot fully solve. Keep language clear for the stated level.

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
