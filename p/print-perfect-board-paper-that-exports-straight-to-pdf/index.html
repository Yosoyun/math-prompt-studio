Print-Perfect Board Paper That Exports Straight to PDF

A full, properly-sectioned board exam paper ready to paste into Word and print as a clean PDF.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT (free) or Claude (free), then paste into Google Docs/Word

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Fill every [PLACEHOLDER] with your real details.
  2. 2. Paste chapter list or syllabus under INPUT.
  3. 3. Copy the reply into Google Docs.
  4. 4. Set page size A4, then Export as PDF.
  5. 5. Spot-check 3-4 answers before printing.
  6. For a trustworthy answer key: paste the finished paper into a FRESH chat and ask it to solve and grade it as an unseen exam (it catches its own slips).
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If marks don't add up, type: 'The section marks do not total [X]. Recount each question, fix the totals, and reprint the full paper with a corrected mark distribution table.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a senior board examination paper-setter with 25 years of experience setting and moderating papers for school boards.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] [SUBJECT] for [BOARD e.g. CBSE/ICSE/State]. I need one complete, ready-to-print examination paper. Total marks: [TOTAL MARKS]. Duration: [DURATION]. Difficulty mix I want: roughly [e.g. 30% easy, 50% moderate, 20% hard].

YOUR TASK:
1. Build a complete paper with a clean header block: school name line, exam name, subject, class, time, max marks, and a 'General Instructions' list (5-7 numbered instructions).
2. Organise into standard sections (e.g. Section A objective/MCQ, Section B very short, Section C short, Section D long, Section E case/source-based) appropriate to [BOARD].
3. Number every question continuously and show marks in brackets at the right of each question, e.g. (1), (3), (5).
4. Include internal choice ('OR') where the board pattern expects it.
5. Add a final one-line mark-distribution table: section, number of questions, marks each, section total, and grand total.

INPUT: Here is the syllabus / chapter list to draw from: [PASTE CHAPTERS OR TOPICS]. Use ONLY these topics.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: First solve every numerical/answerable question silently to confirm it is solvable and unambiguous. VERIFY by two independent checks: (a) re-add all section marks and confirm they equal [TOTAL MARKS]; (b) confirm question count and difficulty mix match what I asked. Never invent formulas, theorems, or out-of-syllabus topics. Keep all mathematics fully legible in plain text: write fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, square roots as sqrt(x), and label figures in words where a diagram is implied.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1. Header block.
2. General Instructions.
3. Section-by-section questions with marks.
4. Mark-distribution table.
5. A short note: 'Total verified = [TOTAL MARKS]'.
Use simple lines and spacing so it pastes cleanly into Word/Google Docs and prints as A4 PDF. Do not use heavy tables that break on paste; keep the mark table as plain rows.

RULES: Stay strictly inside the given syllabus. No answer key here (that is a separate document). Keep wording exam-formal and unambiguous. Ensure every question has a single clear interpretation.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the header block under the exam name.
