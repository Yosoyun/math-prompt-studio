Fast Feedback Comment Bank

A ready bank of short, specific feedback comments grouped by error type.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt below.
  3. 3. Type in the chapter or topic.
  4. 4. Send and skim the comment groups.
  5. 5. Keep the list open and copy comments as you mark.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If comments are too generic, reply: "Make each comment point to a specific maths mistake and tell the student the one thing to do next - no vague praise like 'good effort'."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a master mathematics teacher who is famous for fast, kind, useful written feedback.

CONTEXT: I teach Class [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I am marking a large set of answer scripts on the topic [TOPIC or CHAPTER]. I want a bank of short comments I can reuse so feedback is specific, consistent and quick to write.

YOUR TASK:
1. Think about the typical mistakes and strengths students show in this topic.
2. Produce a comment bank organised into clear groups: Conceptual errors, Calculation and sign errors, Method and presentation, Missing steps or justification, and Praise for things done well.
3. In each group give 6 to 10 short comments. Each comment must (a) name the issue precisely and (b) give one concrete next step.
4. Keep every comment under 25 words so it fits in a margin.
5. Add a small set of 5 encouraging closing remarks for the bottom of a script.

INPUT: Topic or chapter being marked: [TOPIC or CHAPTER]. Anything specific you want covered: [OPTIONAL - PASTE A COMMON MISTAKE YOU KEEP SEEING].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Draft the comments internally first, then VERIFY by two checks: (a) every comment names a real, specific maths issue in this topic, not a generic platitude, and (b) every corrective comment contains one clear action the student can take. Never invent rules or formulas. Keep maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT, in this exact order:
1. TITLE BLOCK with topic, class and the line "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav".
2. The five comment groups as headed bullet lists.
3. CLOSING REMARKS (5 short encouraging lines).
4. HOW TO USE: two lines on how to mix one strength comment with one corrective comment per script.

RULES: No empty praise. No comment longer than 25 words. Every corrective comment must give a next step. Use simple, warm, professional language suitable for a student to read.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
