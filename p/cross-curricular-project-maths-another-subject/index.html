Cross-Curricular Project (Maths + Another Subject)

A project linking maths to science, art, history or geography, with combined rubric.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Fill in maths topic, partner subject, class.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt into any AI chat.
  3. 3. Check how the two subjects link.
  4. 4. Copy the brief into a document.
  5. 5. Grade with the combined rubric.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the link feels forced, reply: "Make the connection between maths and the other subject more genuine and central, and re-verify any calculations."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a maths teacher who designs cross-curricular projects that link mathematics meaningfully to another subject.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board]. I want a CROSS-CURRICULAR PROJECT linking the maths topic [MATHS TOPIC e.g. ratios / geometry / data] with the subject [OTHER SUBJECT e.g. Science / Art / History / Geography / Music]. The maths must be central, not decoration.

YOUR TASK:
1. State the genuine link between the maths topic and the other subject.
2. Give a student-facing project goal and concrete deliverable.
3. Provide numbered steps showing exactly where the maths is used.
4. Include one worked example demonstrating the maths in this context.
5. Provide a combined rubric (20 marks) covering both the maths and the other-subject content.
6. Add 2 differentiation ideas.

INPUT: Here is my maths topic and the partner subject: [PASTE MATHS TOPIC AND OTHER SUBJECT HERE]. If blank, choose a strong pairing for the class and board above.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Plan internally and ensure the maths does real work in the project. VERIFY any calculation two ways and show both checks. Keep facts from the other subject accurate; if unsure of a fact, say so rather than inventing it. Never invent formulas. Keep all maths legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings in this order):
1. PROJECT TITLE
2. THE MATHS-AND-[SUBJECT] LINK
3. PROJECT GOAL AND DELIVERABLE
4. STUDENT STEPS (numbered; mark where the maths is used)
5. WORKED EXAMPLE WITH TWO CHECKS
6. COMBINED RUBRIC (20 marks; show which marks are maths and which are the other subject)
7. DIFFERENTIATION (2 ideas)
8. TEACHER TIPS

RULES: The maths must be essential. Be specific and accurate. Short, plain sentences.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
