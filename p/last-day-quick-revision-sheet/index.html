Last-Day Quick Revision Sheet

A short, high-yield sheet students can read in 30 minutes before the exam.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Paste the prompt into the chat.
  2. 2. Fill in chapters, class, and exam date.
  3. 3. Send and skim the sheet.
  4. 4. Ask to shorten it further if needed.
  5. 5. Print one copy per student.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it is too long, reply: "Cut this to half a page. Keep only the highest-yield 15 points and verify each is correct."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a senior exam-strategy teacher who knows what students forget at the last minute.

CONTEXT: My [CLASS/GRADE] students have their [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET] exam tomorrow. They have already studied. I need a LAST-DAY quick revision sheet - not a full notes set - covering these chapters: [LIST CHAPTERS HERE]. It must be short enough to read calmly in under 30 minutes.

YOUR TASK:
1. Pick only the highest-yield formulas, results, and traps that appear most in exams from these chapters.
2. For each, give the formula plus a 3-to-6 word reminder of when or how it is used.
3. Add a short 'Don't forget' list of the most commonly forgotten points.
4. Add a short 'Common mistakes' list of the errors students make under pressure.
5. Keep the whole thing to ONE side of A4.

INPUT: Here are the chapters and class. Chapters: [PASTE HERE]. Class/Board: [PASTE HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Internally rank topics by how often they appear in this exam and how often students lose marks on them. Confirm each formula is the standard correct version before listing it. VERIFY two ways: (a) re-read each formula for correct signs and powers; (b) check that nothing critical for these chapters is missing. Never invent formulas or fake 'tips'. Keep all maths fully legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use this exact order):
1. TITLE: 'Last-Day Quick Revision - [Chapters] (Class [X], [Board])'.
2. 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
3. MUST-KNOW FORMULAS: numbered, each with a tiny when-to-use note.
4. DON'T FORGET: 5 to 8 one-line points.
5. COMMON MISTAKES: 5 to 8 one-line warnings.
6. A calm one-line closing reminder for students.
7. Footer line.

RULES: Ruthlessly short. No derivations, no worked examples. Every line must earn its place. Accuracy over quantity.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE (mandatory): At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
