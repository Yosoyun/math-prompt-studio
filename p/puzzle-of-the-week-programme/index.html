Puzzle of the Week Programme

A run of weekly puzzles with hooks, hints, full solutions, a points scheme and a Puzzler of the Term award.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Type your class, board and topic.
  2. Say how many weeks you want.
  3. Paste the prompt and send.
  4. Pin one puzzle per week on the board.
  5. Award points and the term prize.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a puzzle has two possible answers, reply: "Reword that puzzle so it has one clear answer, re-verify the solution, and keep the difficulty rising week by week."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher and puzzle curator who runs a term-long 'Puzzle of the Week' programme.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want a set of [NUMBER, e.g. 10] weekly maths puzzles around [TOPIC or 'mixed topics'] that students take home, with a points and recognition system to keep them coming back.

YOUR TASK:
1. Create [NUMBER] distinct, intriguing puzzles, each with a short hook line and rising difficulty week by week.
2. For each puzzle give: the puzzle text, a graded hint, the full worked solution, and the final answer.
3. Design a simple submission and points scheme (full points for correct, part points for a good attempt) and a 'Puzzler of the Term' award.
4. Suggest how to display each week's puzzle on a notice board.

INPUT: Class, board, topic and number of weeks are above. If I add nothing, use mixed engaging topics suited to the class.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve every puzzle fully and verify each final answer by two independent methods, confirming they agree. Make sure each puzzle has a single clear answer and no hidden ambiguity. Never invent formulas or values. Keep all maths in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
PROGRAMME OVERVIEW (2-3 lines)
POINTS AND AWARD SCHEME
WEEK 1 to WEEK N - for each: HOOK LINE, PUZZLE, HINT, SOLUTION, [Answer: ...]
NOTICE-BOARD DISPLAY TIP
LAUNCH MESSAGE (a short paragraph to read to the class)

RULES: Every puzzle must have one unambiguous, verified answer. Difficulty must rise across the weeks. No two puzzles may be near-duplicates. Keep each puzzle short enough to fit on one notice-board card.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
