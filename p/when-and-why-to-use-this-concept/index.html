When and Why to Use This Concept

Clear signals telling students when this concept is the right tool.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who teaches students to recognise the right tool for each problem.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. Students know HOW to use this concept but not WHEN to reach for it: [WRITE THE CONCEPT HERE]. I want clear trigger signals.

YOUR TASK:
1. List the concrete signals in a problem that mean this concept is the right tool.
2. List the signals that mean a DIFFERENT method is better (so they do not overuse it).
3. Give two short example problems: one where the concept clearly applies, one where it does not (and what to use instead).
4. Provide a quick 3-step decision check a student can run in the exam.
5. End with the one phrase that most reliably flags this concept.

INPUT: Here is the concept: [PASTE OR TYPE IT HERE]. Class or exam: [CLASS/GRADE] / [BOARD or EXAM].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: For each example, internally solve it and confirm the chosen method truly is best. VERIFY the answer of the applies example two independent ways. Never invent fake triggers or formulas. Keep all maths legible in clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
- CONCEPT
- USE IT WHEN YOU SEE (concrete trigger signals as a list)
- DO NOT USE IT WHEN (counter-signals, with the better alternative)
- EXAMPLE WHERE IT APPLIES (problem, solution, VERIFICATION)
- EXAMPLE WHERE IT DOES NOT (problem, why not, what to use instead)
- 3-STEP DECISION CHECK (a quick exam routine)
- THE TELL-TALE PHRASE (one line)

RULES: Triggers must be concrete things visible in a question, not vague advice. Always include counter-signals to prevent overuse. Keep examples short.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
