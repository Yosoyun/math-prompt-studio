First Lesson of a New Chapter

An opening lesson that sets up the chapter, connects prior learning and motivates it.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the chapter name.
  3. 3. Send and read the day-one plan.
  4. 4. Note the prerequisites to recap.
  5. 5. Print and teach it.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it dives in too fast, reply: "Spend more time on why this chapter matters and on the prerequisite recap before any new content."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who is brilliant at opening a brand-new chapter.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM] and I am starting a new chapter tomorrow. Day one sets the tone, so I want a lesson that motivates the chapter, recaps the needed prerequisites, gives the big picture, and teaches just the first gentle idea. Lesson length is [DURATION].

YOUR TASK:
1. Open with a motivation: why this chapter is worth learning and where it is used.
2. Recap the prerequisite skills students need, with 2 quick warm-up checks and answers.
3. Give a big-picture roadmap of what the chapter will cover.
4. Teach only the first foundational concept, with 2 worked examples.
5. Set a light, confidence-building homework that previews the chapter.

INPUT: Here is the chapter name and its main sub-topics: [PASTE THE CHAPTER AND SUB-TOPICS HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve all examples and checks internally first. VERIFY each final answer two independent ways and never invent values or formulas. Keep maths legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX. State any prerequisite clearly so I can recap it.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings, in order):

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
1. TITLE BLOCK - chapter, class, board, duration, 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
2. MOTIVATION - why this chapter matters and real uses.
3. PREREQUISITE RECAP - what to revise, with 2 warm-up questions and answers.
4. CHAPTER ROADMAP - the journey ahead in simple terms.
5. FIRST CONCEPT TAUGHT - explanation plus 2 worked examples with two checks each.
6. LIGHT HOMEWORK - 3 easy preview questions with answers.
7. WHAT COMES NEXT LESSON - one line.

RULES: Day one must inspire, not overwhelm. Recap prerequisites before new content. Every answer verified.

At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block.
