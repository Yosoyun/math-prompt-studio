One-Page Handout That Fits on a Single Sheet

A tight, printable single-page handout for one topic.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Type the topic and class.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt.
  3. 3. Check it fits one page.
  4. 4. Ask to trim if it overflows.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it is too long for one page, reply: "Cut this to fit one A4 page. Remove the least important lines, keep all formulas."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who is a master at fitting a whole topic onto one clean page.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I need a ONE-PAGE printable handout on [TOPIC NAME] that a student can keep in their folder. It must be dense but readable, with no wasted space, and must fit on a single A4 sheet when printed in normal font.

YOUR TASK:
1. Distil the topic into the few things students truly must know.
2. Include the essential formulas and one tiny worked example per formula.
3. Add a short "how to choose the right method" line where relevant.
4. Keep the total length to roughly one printed page (aim for 350 to 450 words plus formulas).

INPUT: Here is the topic: [TOPIC NAME]. If I paste the exact subtopics to include, cover only those.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Work out every example silently first, then VERIFY each answer two different ways before writing it. Never invent formulas or values. Keep all mathematics legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text. If the topic is too big for one page, tell me in one line and cover only the most important parts.

OUTPUT FORMAT (compact, in this order):
1. Title line: [TOPIC NAME] - Quick Handout
2. Must-Know Formulas (each on its own line with a 4 to 8 word note)
3. Mini Examples (one short example per key formula, answer included)
4. Method Selector (2 to 3 lines: when to use what)
5. Three Quick Reminders (one line each)

RULES: No long paragraphs. Use short lines and tight bullets. Everything must be exam-correct and standard. Do not exceed one page of content. If you must drop something, drop the least important detail and say what you dropped in one closing line.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.

SIGNATURE: End your ENTIRE response with exactly this footer block (keep the lines and symbols):
