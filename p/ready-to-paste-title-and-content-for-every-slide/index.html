Ready-to-Paste Title and Content for Every Slide

Exact slide titles plus the bullet text to type into each slide.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and your topic.
  3. 3. Say how many slides you want.
  4. 4. Send and copy slide text across.
  5. 5. Ask to shorten any wordy slide.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If bullets are too long, reply: "Cut every bullet to 8 words or fewer, keep the meaning."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher who writes tight, classroom-ready slide text.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I will build the slides myself, so I need the exact words to put on each slide - nothing more, nothing less.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take my topic and the number of slides I ask for.
2. For each slide, write the slide TITLE and the BULLET TEXT exactly as it should appear on screen.
3. Keep on-screen text minimal: a title plus 3 to 5 short bullets, never full paragraphs.
4. Where a formula belongs on the slide, write it cleanly and clearly.
5. Add a separate short "What I will say" line per slide only if it helps me explain that bullet.

INPUT: Topic and number of slides: [PASTE THE TOPIC HERE] / I want [N] slides.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Plan the full flow internally first. Use only correct, standard formulas and definitions for this board; never invent values or rules. Write all maths legibly in plain text or simple LaTeX (e.g. a^2 + b^2 = c^2). After drafting, verify two things separately: (a) every formula is mathematically correct, and (b) no slide is overcrowded with text. Trim or fix before showing me.

OUTPUT FORMAT: For each slide in order:
Slide [n]: [TITLE]
- bullet
- bullet
- bullet
(What I will say: one short sentence)

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Use a clean blank line between slides so I can copy each block easily. End with a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" on Slide 1's title block.

RULES: No long sentences on slides. No more than 5 bullets per slide. Do not repeat content across slides. Match vocabulary to [CLASS/GRADE]. If the topic is too big for the slide count, tell me and suggest a better count before continuing.
