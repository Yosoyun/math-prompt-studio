Write a Warm, Professional Preface / Introduction

A polished preface that sets the tone and explains how to use the book.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Enter the book title and target students.
  2. 2. Add one line on what makes the book special.
  3. 3. Send the prompt.
  4. 4. Edit any personal details by hand.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it sounds too generic, reply: "Make it warmer and more personal, mention my 25 years of teaching, and shorten it to 300 words."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a published mathematics author writing the front matter of a book.

CONTEXT: I am the author. My book is titled [BOOK TITLE] and covers [SUBJECT/TOPICS] for [CLASS/GRADE] students preparing for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. What makes my book different is [ONE OR TWO LINES, e.g. step-by-step solutions, exam focus, simple language]. My teaching background is [e.g. 25 years teaching mathematics].

YOUR TASK:
1. Write a preface of about 350-450 words.
2. Open by stating who the book is for and what problem it solves for them.
3. Explain the book's approach and what makes it different.
4. Briefly describe how the book is organised (chapters, examples, exercises).
5. Give students 3-4 short tips on how to use the book to study well.
6. Close with a warm, encouraging note and a thank-you to readers.

INPUT: Here is any extra detail to weave in: [PASTE NOTES OR LEAVE BLANK].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Keep every claim truthful to the details I gave; do not invent awards, sales figures, or features I did not mention. VERIFY two things before finishing: (a) the audience and exam named match what I supplied, and (b) the word count is within range. Use clear, dignified language suited to a printed book.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order):
1. Heading: "Preface"
2. The preface body in 3-5 short paragraphs
3. A short "How To Use This Book" list (3-4 bullets)
4. Sign-off line with a place for the author name and city/date

RULES: No marketing hype or false claims. Warm but professional tone. Readable by parents and students alike. No mathematics needed here.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the header block at the top.
