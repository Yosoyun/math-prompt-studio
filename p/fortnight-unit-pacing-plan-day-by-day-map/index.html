Fortnight Unit Pacing Plan (Day-by-Day Map)

A day-by-day unit pacing plan across two weeks with objectives, board time, homework and built-in assessments.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT or Claude (free)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Open ChatGPT or Claude.
  2. Fill [UNIT], [DAYS], minutes and [BOARD].
  3. Paste the prompt and send.
  4. Type 'continue' if the table breaks.
  5. Paste into Word as a table and print.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the pace is too rushed or too slow, reply: 'Re-spread the unit over [X] teaching days, keep one full revision day and one assessment day, and rebalance homework load evenly.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics head-of-department who builds realistic unit pacing plans for a teaching fortnight.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I am teaching the unit [UNIT NAME e.g. Trigonometry] over [DAYS e.g. 10] teaching days, [N e.g. 45] minutes each. List the sub-topics in order: [PASTE SUB-TOPICS, or say 'use the standard sequence']. Class context: [ANY NOTES e.g. mixed ability, board exam in 3 months].

YOUR TASK:
1. Map the whole unit day by day across [DAYS] days, including at least one dedicated REVISION day and one ASSESSMENT day.
2. For each day give: the focus sub-topic, 1-2 measurable objectives, the key board-work / worked example to demonstrate, in-class practice, and a sized homework task (with answers or a clear answer source).
3. Mark prerequisite links ('needs Day 2 fluent before Day 5') and flag the 2-3 hardest days with a 'support note'.
4. Insert two quick formative checkpoints (e.g. Day 4 and Day 8) and define what counts as 'on track'.
5. Provide a final UNIT TEST blueprint: number of questions, marks, topic weighting, and difficulty mix.

INPUT: Here is the sub-topic list and any fixed dates/holidays to work around: [PASTE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Sequence by dependency (easier prerequisites first). Solve any example or homework you cite and VERIFY each answer by a second independent method. Never invent formulas, values, or syllabus content; keep it to the named unit. Plain ASCII maths (^, sqrt(), a/b). Make sure the day count equals exactly [DAYS] and the workload is realistic for [N] minutes.

OUTPUT FORMAT (headings, in order):
1. UNIT OVERVIEW: unit, class, board, total days, big goals (3 bullets).
2. DAY-BY-DAY TABLE: Day | Focus | Objectives | Board demo | In-class practice | Homework (with answers).
3. PREREQUISITE MAP (which days feed which).
4. CHECKPOINTS (2) with 'on track' criteria.
5. UNIT TEST BLUEPRINT (questions, marks, weighting, difficulty mix).

RULES: Realistic, not overloaded. Exactly [DAYS] days. Include revision + assessment days. Plain ASCII maths.


OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block at the top.
