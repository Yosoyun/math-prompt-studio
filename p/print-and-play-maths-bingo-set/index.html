Print-and-Play Maths Bingo Set

A 30-problem master list plus several unique 5x5 answer cards and a ready calling list.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Type your topic, class and board.
  2. Say how many bingo cards you want.
  3. Paste the prompt and send.
  4. Copy each 5x5 card onto paper.
  5. Read the call list; students mark answers.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If two answers clash or a card looks identical to another, reply: "Make every answer unique, re-verify them, and rebuild the cards so no two win at the same time."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher and bingo-card designer who builds a print-and-play maths bingo set.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want a maths bingo game on [TOPIC] where I read out a problem and students mark the matching ANSWER on their card.

YOUR TASK:
1. Create a master list of 30 problems on [TOPIC] with their final answers.
2. From those 30 answers, design [NUMBER, e.g. 6] DIFFERENT 5x5 bingo cards, each with a FREE centre square, each using a different mix of 24 answers so no two cards win at the same moment.
3. Provide the teacher's CALL LIST: the 30 problems in a good calling order, each tagged with its answer.
4. State the win condition (line, full house, or four corners) and how to verify a winner.

INPUT: Topic, class and board are above. Tell me how many cards you want here: [NUMBER OF CARDS].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve all 30 problems first. Verify each answer by two independent methods and confirm agreement. Make sure every answer is unique so marking is unambiguous; if two problems share an answer, change one. Keep all maths in clean LaTeX or clear plain text.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
HOW TO PLAY (numbered, including win condition)
MASTER PROBLEM-AND-ANSWER LIST (all 30, answers in boxes [Answer: ...])
BINGO CARDS (print each as a labelled 5x5 grid with FREE in the centre)
TEACHER CALL LIST (problems in calling order, each with its answer)
HOW TO CHECK A WINNER (quick steps)

RULES: Every answer on every card must come from the master list. No two cards may be identical. All answers must be distinct and verified. Keep numbers clean enough to fit a grid cell.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
