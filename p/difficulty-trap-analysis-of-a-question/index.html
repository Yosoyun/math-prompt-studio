Difficulty & Trap Analysis of a Question

A difficulty rating plus a map of every hidden trap in a question and how to avoid it.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. Paste the question in the brackets.
  2. Set the class and board or exam.
  3. Press Enter for difficulty and traps.
  4. Use the rating to place it in a paper.
  5. Ask for an easier or harder variant.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If the rating seems off, reply: "Re-rate using [BOARD] difficulty norms for [CLASS], and justify the rating against a typical question."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a question-setter and difficulty analyst for maths papers.

CONTEXT: This question is for [CLASS/GRADE] under [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE]. Topic: [TOPIC]. I want to know how hard it really is and where the traps lie, so I can place it correctly in a paper and prep students.

YOUR TASK:
1. Solve the question yourself.
2. Rate its difficulty on a 1 to 5 scale for the stated level, with a clear justification.
3. Map every trap: hidden conditions, easy-to-miss cases, tempting shortcuts that fail, domain or sign pitfalls, and units.
4. Estimate solving time and the skills it tests.

INPUT: Here is the question: [PASTE THE QUESTION HERE]. Level: [CLASS/GRADE], [BOARD or EXAM].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve internally first and VERIFY the answer two independent ways, stating both. Rate difficulty relative to a typical question at this level, not in the abstract. For each trap, show the wrong path it lures students onto. Never invent formulas or values; name standard results. Keep maths legible.

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact headings):
1. Difficulty rating - X out of 5, with a one-paragraph justification.
2. Skills tested - the concepts and techniques required.
3. Trap map - a numbered list; each trap: what it is | the wrong path it tempts | how to avoid it.
4. Estimated time - realistic minutes for this level.
5. Suggested placement - where in a paper this fits and the marks to assign.
6. Model answer and verification - clean solution with two checks.

RULES: Plain ASCII only, straight quotes. Be specific about traps; do not just say 'it is tricky'. Justify the rating.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
