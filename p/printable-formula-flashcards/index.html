Printable Formula Flashcards

Front-and-back flashcard text you can print, cut, and fold.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Paste the prompt into the chat.
  2. 2. Enter chapter and class.
  3. 3. Send and review the cards.
  4. 4. Ask for more cards if any topic is thin.
  5. 5. Paste into a document, print, and cut.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a card's back does not match its front, reply: "Card [number] front and back don't match. Fix it and verify the formula on the back."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a mathematics teacher building printable revision flashcards.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for the [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / ICSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. I want FORMULA FLASHCARDS for [CHAPTER NAME] that I can print, cut, and give to students for self-testing. The output is text laid out as front/back card pairs.

YOUR TASK:
1. Create one flashcard per important formula in the chapter.
2. FRONT of card: a short cue - the formula's name, or a 'What is the formula for...?' question, or a 'when to use' situation.
3. BACK of card: the exact formula, plus a one-line note or condition if needed.
4. Number the cards so front and back stay paired when printed.
5. Keep each card short enough to fit a small printed card.

INPUT: Here is the chapter and class. Chapter: [PASTE HERE]. Class/Board: [PASTE HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Internally confirm each formula on each card back is the standard correct version, and that the front cue clearly points to that formula. VERIFY two ways: (a) re-read each back for symbol, sign, and power accuracy; (b) confirm each front truly matches its back. Never invent a formula. Keep all maths fully legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX so it prints cleanly.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use this exact order):
1. TITLE: '[Chapter] - Formula Flashcards (Class [X], [Board])'.
2. 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav'.
3. A clear two-column or labelled list: 'Card N - FRONT:' then 'Card N - BACK:'.
4. A short 'How to print and use' note of 2 to 3 lines (print double-sided or fold).
5. Footer line.

RULES: Fronts and backs must match by number. Backs must be exam-accurate. Keep cards compact. No long explanations.

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE (mandatory): At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
