Bilingual English + Hindi Exam Paper (Side-by-Side, Exam-Accurate)

Every question in English with a faithful Hindi version beneath it, numbers and symbols kept identical.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  ChatGPT (free) or Claude (free)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. State class, board, chapters, marks.
  2. 2. Ask English line then Hindi line per Q.
  3. 3. Check technical Hindi terms read naturally.
  4. 4. Confirm numbers match in both languages.
  5. 5. Paste into Word, export A4 PDF.
  6. For a trustworthy answer key: paste the finished paper into a FRESH chat and ask it to solve and grade it as an unseen exam (it catches its own slips).
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If Hindi maths terms sound off, type: 'Use standard NCERT Hindi terminology (e.g. bahupad for polynomial, samikaran for equation) and keep all numbers and symbols in international form in both languages.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a bilingual board paper-setter who prepares exam papers in English and Hindi to official standards.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] [SUBJECT] for [BOARD, e.g. CBSE/State]. I need a single bilingual paper where every question appears first in English, then in faithful Hindi directly beneath it, exactly as in real board papers. Total marks: [TOTAL MARKS]. Duration: [DURATION].

YOUR TASK:
1. Build a complete paper with a bilingual header block (school/exam/subject/class/time/max-marks in English and Hindi) and bilingual general instructions.
2. For every question, print the English version, then on the next line the Hindi version, then the marks in brackets once (shared).
3. Keep ALL numbers, variables, equations and symbols IDENTICAL and in international/Latin form in both languages (do not translate digits, formulas, or variable names).
4. Use standard NCERT-style Hindi mathematical/scientific terminology.
5. End with a mark-distribution table (labels bilingual) and a total check.

INPUT: Chapters/topics to use: [PASTE CHAPTERS]. Use ONLY these.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Solve each question once and confirm the English and Hindi versions ask for exactly the same thing with the same data. VERIFY by two checks: (a) for each question, English and Hindi carry identical numbers, symbols and required answer; (b) section marks re-add to [TOTAL MARKS]. Never mistranslate a quantity or invent a term - if a clean Hindi technical term does not exist, keep the English term in brackets. Keep mathematics legible: fractions a/b, powers x^2, roots sqrt(x).

OUTPUT FORMAT (exact order, per question):
- English line.
- Hindi line.
- (marks)
Preceded by the bilingual header and instructions; followed by the bilingual mark table and a line 'Total verified / kul yog satyapit = [TOTAL MARKS]'.

RULES: Translation must be faithful and exam-formal, never casual. Numbers and symbols stay international form in both languages. No answer key here. Plain formatting so it pastes cleanly into Word and exports as A4 PDF.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.
