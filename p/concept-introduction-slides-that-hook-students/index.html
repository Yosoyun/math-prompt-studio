Concept Introduction Slides That Hook Students

Intro slides that explain a new idea from intuition first, then the formal definition.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the concept.
  3. 3. Send and read the intro flow.
  4. 4. Ask for a real-life example slide.
  5. 5. Copy into your deck.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it jumps to formulas too fast, reply: "Add one more intuition slide before the definition on slide [n]."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a master mathematics teacher famous for making new concepts click instantly.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. I am introducing a brand-new concept for the first time. I need introduction slides that build intuition before any formal definition, so students understand WHY before they memorise.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the concept I give you.
2. Start with a hook: a relatable question, a real-life situation, or a puzzle the concept solves.
3. Build intuition step by step with a simple example, no heavy notation yet.
4. Then give the precise formal definition and the key formula.
5. End with one slide that connects the new idea to something students already know.
6. Mark any slide that needs a picture or graph as [VISUAL: describe it].

INPUT: New concept to introduce: [PASTE THE CONCEPT HERE]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Plan the intuition-to-formal flow internally. Use only correct, standard definitions and notation for this board; never invent terms. Keep all maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. Before finishing, verify two things separately: (a) the formal definition you give is standard and exactly correct, and (b) the simple example you used genuinely illustrates the concept (test it with real numbers). Fix any mismatch.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Slides in order, each as:
Slide [n]: [TITLE]
- short bullets
[VISUAL: ...] where needed.
Label the slide types in brackets: (Hook), (Intuition), (Definition), (Connection).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
Put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" on Slide 1; end with footer "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: No formula before intuition. Language must suit [CLASS/GRADE]. Keep each slide short and screen-friendly. The hook must be genuinely interesting, not generic.
