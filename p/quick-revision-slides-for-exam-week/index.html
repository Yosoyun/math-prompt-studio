Quick Revision Slides for Exam Week

Fast revision slides packed with formulas, traps and one-line memory aids.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open a free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the prompt and the chapter name.
  3. 3. Send and review the formula slides.
  4. 4. Ask for more common-mistake slides if needed.
  5. 5. Copy into your deck.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If a formula looks wrong, reply: "Recheck the formula on slide [n] and verify it with one worked example."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a senior mathematics teacher who makes razor-sharp last-minute revision slides.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] for [BOARD or EXAM]. My students have exams soon and need fast, high-yield revision slides for one chapter - all the must-know points in the smallest number of slides.

YOUR TASK:
1. Take the chapter I give you.
2. Produce revision slides covering: key definitions, all important formulas, standard results, common mistakes, and 3 to 4 quick self-test questions with answers.
3. Group formulas logically so they are easy to memorise.
4. Add one short memory tip or trick per formula slide where it genuinely helps.
5. Keep it dense but readable - this is for revision, not first teaching.

INPUT: Chapter to revise: [PASTE THE CHAPTER NAME OR SYLLABUS POINTS HERE]

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Recall the chapter internally and list every exam-relevant point. Use only correct, standard formulas for this board; do not invent shortcuts. Keep all maths legible in plain text or simple LaTeX. Verify every formula two independent ways before including it: (1) check it against a known special case or simple example, and (2) re-derive or cross-check it mentally. Remove anything you cannot confirm.

OUTPUT FORMAT:

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
- Slide 1: Chapter title + "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav".
- Formula slides (grouped, with memory tips).
- One "Common Mistakes" slide (mistake -> correct way).
- One "Quick Self-Test" slide (questions) + an "Answers" slide.
- Each slide as: Slide [n]: [TITLE] then bullets.
- End with footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav".

RULES: No teaching from scratch - assume the chapter was already taught. Pack value, not fluff. Match the [BOARD or EXAM] syllabus exactly. Flag any point that is often examined with the tag (HIGH YIELD).

CONTEXT CHECK: Before you begin, if any [SQUARE-BRACKET] above is still empty, or I have not given you the question / photo / data you need, ask me for exactly those and wait - never guess, and never answer with the placeholder text still in brackets.

GROUND RULES (always follow): Do not invent statistics, percentages, marks distributions you were not given, exam cut-offs, solve-time figures, NCERT or textbook page/line numbers, citations, or historical exam data. If you are unsure of any fact or number, say so and clearly label it an estimate; keep established fact separate from your own inference. Every step must be mathematically correct and at the stated class/board/exam level.

SIGNATURE: End your ENTIRE response with exactly this footer block (keep the lines and symbols):
