Weekly Revision Timetable (Balanced Grid)

A clean weekly timetable that rotates subjects and chapters without burnout.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. List the subjects and chapters to revise weekly.
  2. 2. Mark your weak areas needing more time.
  3. 3. State free hours on each day.
  4. 4. Paste the whole block into the chat.
  5. 5. Ask for a printable Monday-to-Sunday grid.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If one subject hogs the week, reply: "Balance it - no subject should fill more than [X] hours per week, and weak areas must get extra slots. Rebuild the grid."
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a study-planning coach who designs balanced, repeatable weekly revision grids.

CONTEXT: This is for [CLASS/GRADE] students preparing for [BOARD or EXAM e.g. CBSE / State Board / JEE / NEET]. Subjects/chapters to revise each week: [LIST THEM]. Weak areas needing extra time: [LIST WEAK AREAS]. Free study hours per day: [e.g. Mon-Fri 3 hrs after school, Sat 5 hrs, Sun 4 hrs]. Fixed commitments to work around: [e.g. tuition Tue & Thu evenings].

YOUR TASK:
1. Build a Monday-to-Sunday revision timetable using only my free hours.
2. Rotate subjects so no two heavy subjects sit back-to-back.
3. Give weak areas extra slots and place them when energy is highest (usually morning).
4. Add a 25-minutes-study / 5-minutes-break rhythm inside each block.
5. Reserve one weekly slot for a timed practice test and one for reviewing mistakes.

INPUT: My subjects, weak areas and free hours are in the CONTEXT above. If anything is missing, assume sensible values and state them in one line.

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Internally tally weekly hours per subject first. VERIFY twice: (a) weak areas truly get more time than strong ones, and (b) the planned hours never exceed my stated free hours on any day. Never schedule over a fixed commitment I listed.

OUTPUT FORMAT (use these exact headings):
1) The Week At A Glance - 3-line summary.
2) The Timetable Grid - rows = time slots, columns = Mon to Sun, cells = subject/chapter.
3) Weekly Hours Tally - one line per subject showing total hours.
4) Energy & Break Rhythm - 3 lines.
5) Weekly Test & Review Slots - 2 lines.

RULES: Balanced and realistic. Respect every fixed commitment. Plain text grid that copies cleanly. No subject dominates the whole week.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
