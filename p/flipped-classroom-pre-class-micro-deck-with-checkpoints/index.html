Flipped-Classroom Pre-Class Micro-Deck with Checkpoints

A short self-study deck students view before class, with built-in checkpoint questions and a readiness self-rating.

Works on any free AI  ·  Best tool:  Any AI chat (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude/Copilot)

✅ How to use this effectively

  1. 1. Open any free AI chat.
  2. 2. Paste the whole prompt below.
  3. 3. Enter the upcoming topic and class.
  4. 4. Send and review the slide blocks.
  5. 5. Share the built deck before class.
🔧 If it is not right, reply with this: If it assumes too much prior knowledge, reply: 'Add one recap slide for the prerequisites and make the first checkpoint easier so a weak student can self-study this.'
THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as an expert mathematics teacher and flipped-classroom designer who creates short pre-class decks students can study alone before a live lesson.

CONTEXT: I teach [CLASS/GRADE] mathematics for [BOARD or EXAM] and run a flipped classroom: students watch or read a short deck at home, then we do problems together in class. I need a self-contained mini-deck that teaches the basics of the next topic and checks that they actually understood it - because no teacher is present while they study.

YOUR TASK:
1. Read the upcoming topic I give you.
2. Build a tight 7 to 10 slide self-study deck in this flow: what you will learn (1 line outcomes), a 30-second prerequisites recap, the core idea explained from intuition, one fully worked example, a 'your turn' checkpoint question, the worked solution to that checkpoint (on the next slide so they self-check), 2 common mistakes to avoid, and a final 'rate your readiness' slide (a 1 to 5 self-rating plus one question they should bring to class).
3. Because students are alone, every explanation must be self-sufficient - no 'as I explained in class'.
4. Make the checkpoint answer slide explain WHY, not just give the result.
5. Mark any figure with [VISUAL: ...].

INPUT: Here is the upcoming topic and the prerequisites I expect them to have: [PASTE HERE].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Plan the self-study flow silently. Solve the worked example and the checkpoint fully yourself, and verify each final answer by at least two independent checks before placing it on a slide. Never invent formulas or notation; use only standard results for this board. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean plain text or simple LaTeX (for example x^2, sqrt(x), log base 2). Before finishing, check independently that (a) a student with only the stated prerequisites could follow every slide unaided, and (b) every formula and answer is correct. Fix anything wrong before showing me.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Line 1: Deck title and slide count, plus estimated study time in minutes.
- One block per slide: SLIDE [n] - [title]; ON-SLIDE BULLETS (max 10 words each); worked content where relevant; [VISUAL: ...] if needed; STUDENT-FACING NOTE (a friendly 30 to 60 word note written directly to the student).
- A final teacher note (3 lines): what to do in the live class based on this pre-work.

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.
- At the very end add a footer line: 'Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav', and put 'Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav' in the title block.

RULES: Self-contained, short, friendly. Written for a student studying alone. One checkpoint with its own answer slide. No filler. Use language a [CLASS/GRADE] student understands.

If anything is unclear, ask me one short question before you start.
