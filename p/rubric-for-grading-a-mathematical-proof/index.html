Rubric for Grading a Mathematical Proof

A proof-grading rubric covering logic, rigour, structure and notation.

THE PROMPT
ROLE: Act as a university-level mathematics professor and olympiad proof-grader.

CONTEXT: I teach Class [CLASS/GRADE] / [BOARD or EXAM e.g. JEE Advanced / Olympiad / ICSE]. I am grading students' written PROOFS, where correct reasoning matters more than the final line. I need a rubric that rewards rigour and logic, not lucky guesses.

YOUR TASK:
1. Restate the proof task cleanly.
2. Write a correct, rigorous model proof yourself.
3. Build a rubric out of [TOTAL MARKS] with criteria suited to proofs, such as: correct setup and assumptions, logical flow and justification of each step, completeness (no gaps or hand-waving), correct use of theorems with conditions checked, clarity of notation, and valid conclusion.
4. Define what counts as a fatal gap that caps the score, versus a minor slip.
5. State how to score a proof that is correct in idea but missing justification.

INPUT: Here is the statement to be proved: [PASTE THE STATEMENT OR THEOREM HERE]. Total marks: [TOTAL MARKS].

HOW TO WORK IT OUT: Construct the model proof internally first. VERIFY it by two independent checks (for example, test the conclusion on a specific case, and confirm every theorem used has its conditions satisfied). Never invent theorems or skip conditions. Keep all mathematics fully legible in clean LaTeX or clear plain text. Ensure the criterion marks add to exactly [TOTAL MARKS].

OUTPUT FORMAT, in this exact order:
1. STATEMENT TO PROVE (clean restatement).
2. MODEL PROOF (rigorous, step by step, with conditions checked).
3. PROOF RUBRIC TABLE: Criterion | What earns the marks | Marks, with a TOTAL row equal to [TOTAL MARKS].
4. FATAL-GAP LIST: errors that cap the maximum achievable score, and the cap.
5. PARTIAL-CREDIT GUIDE: how to score right-idea-but-incomplete proofs.
6. VERIFICATION NOTE: the two checks you used and that they agree.

RULES: Penalise hand-waving and unjustified jumps. Do not give full marks for a correct final statement with a flawed argument. If the statement is false or needs an added condition, say so clearly at the top before grading.

OUTPUT FILE FORMAT: present the complete result as a clean, ready-to-use document I can paste straight into Microsoft Word or Google Docs - a clear bold title, section headings, neatly numbered items, comfortable spacing, and simple tables only where helpful (avoid heavy formatting that breaks on paste). It must also print cleanly to PDF on A4 (File > Print > Save as PDF). Keep all mathematics fully readable (fractions as a/b, powers as x^2, roots as sqrt(x)).

MATHS FORMATTING: Write ALL mathematics so it reads correctly in a normal chat and pastes cleanly into Word or Google Docs. Use plain readable notation - for example a/b for fractions, x^2 for powers, sqrt(x) for roots, and symbols like <=, >=, !=, +-, pi, theta, integral and sum. Do NOT output raw LaTeX or code such as \frac{}{}, \sqrt{}, x^{2}, $...$ or \( ... \) - these appear as broken symbols to a teacher in a free chat.

SIGNATURE: At the very end add a footer line: "Prepared by Indrajeet Yadav", and put "Compiled by Indrajeet Yadav" in the title or header block.
